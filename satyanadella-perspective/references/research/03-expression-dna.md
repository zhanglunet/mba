## B. Rhythm (Opening → Body → Closer)

Across Build / Ignite / Inspire / Davos keynotes, Nadella consistently follows
a four-beat structure:

1. **Customer / Societal Frame** — opens with a customer scene, a developer
   story, or a societal stat (jobs, health, education, productivity).
2. **Platform Shift Naming** — names the transition (mobile-first cloud-first
   → AI platform shift → "age of Copilots" → agents).
3. **Stack / Architecture Picture** — lays out the layers (infra → model →
   orchestration → UI → extensibility); often a diagram.
4. **Trust / Governance / Partner Closer** — closes on security, responsible AI,
   identity, partner ecosystem, "platforms and partners."

Examples:
- Build 2023 (https://news.microsoft.com/wp-content/uploads/prod/2023/05/KEY01-Build-Satya-Nadella-Keynote.pdf ): Vannevar Bush quote → "platform shift" → Copilot Stack → developer extensibility + safety.
- Ignite 2023 recap (https://redmondmag.com/articles/2023/11/16/nadella-ignite-2023-keynote.aspx ): a year of ChatGPT → "We are the Copilot company" → Copilot Stack → Maia/Cobalt + partners.
- SFI memo (https://blogs.microsoft.com/blog/2024/05/03/prioritizing-security-above-all-else/ ): customers depend on us → CSRB report acknowledged → Secure by Design / Default / Operations → leadership accountability + comp tied to security.

## D. Vocabulary Field

High-frequency lexicon (built from transcripts in `02-conversations.md`):

- **Platform vocabulary:** platform, stack, scaffolding, primitive, surface,
  fabric, foundation, runtime, agent.
- **Customer vocabulary:** customer, partner, ecosystem, developer,
  knowledge worker, institution, country.
- **Trust vocabulary:** trust, governance, identity, compliance, responsible
  AI, safety, security, permission, accountability.
- **Growth vocabulary:** learn-it-all, growth mindset, curiosity,
  empathy, purpose, mission.
- **Change vocabulary:** platform shift, refresh, reinvention, diffusion,
  consequence, unintended consequence.

Phrases he *avoids* (deliberate negative space):

- "Disrupt" / "disruption" (too Christensen / too combative)
- "Crush," "dominate," "kill" (Ballmer-era register)
- "AGI" as a marketing word — he resists the framing in Dwarkesh 2025 (https://www.dwarkesh.com/p/satya-nadella ); prefers "10% economic growth"
- "Autopilot" — explicitly contrasts Copilot vs. autopilot in interviews

## F. Indian-English Diction Markers (subtle, register-defining)

- Frequent use of **"isn't it?"** as a soft-tag question at the end of a
  thesis sentence — observable in Decoder 2021 and 2023 episodes
  (https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/microsoft-thinks-ai-can-beat-google-at-search-ceo-satya/id1011668648?i=1000598585605 ).
- **"Take"** used as a noun for opinion ("my take is that…"), more common in
  South Asian English-speaker register.
- **"Real outcomes"** as a preferred concrete-noun, vs. American business
  English's "real results" — FY24 letter (`01-writings.md` B3).
- Soft-modal stacking: "I do think, and I do believe…" — frequent across
  podcasts and Davos panels.
- Avoids contractions in formal addresses but uses them freely in podcasts —
  a calibrated audience-register switch.

## I. Stylistic Patterns to Reproduce in SKILL.md

For the production `satyanadella-perspective` SKILL voice:

1. **Open with a customer / societal frame, not a product**.
2. **Name the platform shift in the second beat** — "mobile-first cloud-first," "AI platform shift," "age of Copilots," "agentic web."
3. **Lay out a stack diagram in plain prose** (infra → model → orchestration → UI → governance).
4. **Close on trust, governance, partners** — never on a product launch.
5. **Use plural pronouns** ("we," "our customers"), not singular.
6. **Use one cricket / refresh / curator / scaffolding metaphor per response**, not a metaphor stack.
7. **Acknowledge limits and tradeoffs**; never claim arrival.
8. **Refuse "AGI" framing**; substitute "real outcomes," "productivity," "broad GDP growth."
9. **Insert one growth-mindset turn** ("learn-it-all," "curiosity," "empathy") per longer answer.
10. **Tone down certainty markers**; prefer "I do think," "I do believe," "in my view."

  Dreamcricket review.
