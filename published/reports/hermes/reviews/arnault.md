## Judge Background

Bernard Arnault is the chairman and CEO of LVMH, the world's largest luxury goods conglomerate (~€86B revenue, 75+ brands). He is the world's foremost practitioner of luxury brand acquisition and scaling. His personal acquisition of ~23% of Hermès shares (2008–2010 via equity swaps, revealed October 2010) — the most dramatic hostile takeover attempt in luxury history — ended in forced divestiture by 2014. He paid a nominal €8M fine. Hermès prevailed.

His evaluation of Hermès must be read in this context: he knows Hermès's brand machinery better than almost any outsider, having studied it intensely as a target. But he is also its most significant historical adversary.

## Overall Commentary

Let me be direct: my history with Hermès makes this evaluation complicated, and I will not pretend otherwise. The fact that I attempted to acquire Hermès and failed is part of the public record.

What I can say with objectivity is this: Hermès has built something in 189 years that LVMH's oldest brands — Louis Vuitton, founded 1854 — have not quite achieved, which is **total brand coherence across every touchpoint, every generation, every market cycle**. The orange box. The carré. The Birkin. The wait. The workshop. It all tells the same story, consistently, for nearly two centuries.

Where I disagree with those who would give Hermès perfect scores: on Category and Leverage. Hermès is the pinnacle of ultra-luxury, but ultra-luxury is a niche. When I think about what defines luxury in the minds of the global consumer — including consumers in China, the US, and emerging markets — it is Louis Vuitton's monogram as much as the Birkin bag. Hermès defines *perfection* within the category; it does not define the category itself.

On Leverage: the scarcity model is elegant but I believe it is a strategic choice that has both created extraordinary value and permanently capped it. A brand that refuses to grow cannot become what it might otherwise be.

**Judge Verdict**: An extraordinary craft heritage and the most coherent identity system in luxury. Category definition is narrower than its mythological status suggests. Score: 40/50. △ Competitive bias acknowledged.
