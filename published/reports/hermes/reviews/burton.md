## Judge Background

Sarah Burton served as Creative Director of Alexander McQueen from 2010 to 2023, inheriting the role following Lee McQueen's death and steering the house through one of the most delicate creative successions in luxury history. She is most publicly known for designing Catherine, Princess of Wales's wedding dress (2011), but her deeper significance is this: she demonstrated that a brand built on one person's singular genius could survive and even deepen after that person's death — through faithfulness to the founder's core vision. Since 2023, she serves as Creative Director of Givenchy (LVMH). Her lens on Hermès is therefore that of someone who has directly grappled with what brand inheritance means across generations.

## Overall Commentary

I came to this evaluation thinking about succession. How does a brand that was built over nearly two centuries continue to be itself — not a museum piece, not a nostalgia act, but a living, working, relevant force — in a world that changes every year?

The answer Hermès has given, and continues to give, is this: **by not trying to be anything other than what it is**.

When Lee McQueen died in 2010, we faced a version of this question at Alexander McQueen — a much shorter history, a very different kind of genius, but the same fundamental challenge: how do you carry something forward without either abandoning it or embalming it? The answer we found was to go deeper into the archive, deeper into the techniques, deeper into the emotional core of what Lee believed about beauty and darkness and craft. To be more McQueen, not less.

Hermès has been doing this for 189 years. Not more Hermès — which would be expansion — but *deeper* Hermès. Every piece made in 2026 is a more refined, more careful execution of the same principles Thierry Hermès committed to in 1837. That is not stagnation. That is mastery.

The Identity score of 10 is the most certain number I have given in any evaluation. The orange box does not need me to explain it. That is what a perfect identity system looks like.

**Judge Verdict**: A living model of brand inheritance across generations. The orange box is the most powerful wordless brand signal in the industry. Score: 44/50. (Note: current role at Givenchy/LVMH disclosed; no direct investment conflict.)
