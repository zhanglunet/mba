## Judge Background

Brunello Cucinelli is the founder and CEO of the eponymous Italian luxury brand known for cashmere knitwear and a philosophy of "humanistic capitalism." Based in Solomeo, a medieval hamlet in Umbria which Cucinelli has restored as his brand's spiritual home, he is perhaps the closest living analog to what Hermès represents: a founder-artisan building a brand with century-scale ambitions, explicitly rejecting growth that compromises craft. His admiration for Hermès is long-documented and genuine; it is the model for what he aspires to build.

## Overall Commentary

I will confess something: when I began building my company in Solomeo, the brand I thought about most was Hermès. Not to imitate it — imitation is the wrong word — but to understand what it means to build something with the intention of it lasting for centuries.

Hermès is the proof that it is possible. That a company can be founded on the belief that making something perfectly is a sufficient purpose — and that if you hold to that belief with enough consistency and enough courage, the world will eventually recognize it and reward it beyond what any growth strategy could achieve.

The hostile takeover attempt by LVMH in 2010 is, in my reading, the most important event in Hermès's recent history — more important than any product launch or financial result. Because Hermès resisted. The family held together. The artisans continued. The orange boxes kept arriving. And today, Hermès's market capitalization exceeds that of the company that tried to acquire it.

What does that tell us? It tells us that the patient conviction of craft, held over generations, is ultimately worth more than the aggressive conviction of capital held over quarters.

That is what I am trying to build. And that is why I give Hermès 46 out of 50.

**Judge Verdict**: The exemplar of craft-origin brand building at the highest level. The only brand that has held its philosophy without compromise across 189 years. Score: 46/50.
