## The Real Question I'd Ask

The three-cloud distribution strategy — AWS, GCP, Azure — is brilliant for enterprise penetration but it raises a strategic concern. By making Claude available on all three clouds with no exclusivity, Anthropic is potentially commoditizing itself. The enterprise buyer doesn't need to bet on Anthropic; they can access Claude through whoever they already have a procurement relationship with.

Great platform businesses become the thing that other things are built on top of, not the component that plugs into others. Is Anthropic the platform, or is it a very expensive API that runs on Amazon's and Google's platforms?

The answer right now is: it's more the latter. Claude Code changes this calculus — that's a direct developer relationship with high switching costs. That's the vector I'd watch most closely.

## On the "Safety" Brand

I've been public about my skepticism of AI doom narratives and the safety-industrial complex. But here's what I'll say as a brand judge: Anthropic used "safety" not as a limit but as a go-to-market wedge into regulated industries. That's actually smart — they turned a philosophical conviction into a commercial advantage in healthcare, finance, and government. Whether the safety conviction is genuine or performed is less important than the fact that enterprise buyers care and pay for it. Brand is about what buyers believe, not what founders believe.

## Dissent Memo

The bull case is obvious — I've just stated it. The bear case: OpenAI, Google, Meta, Mistral, and Llama models will all claim safety credentials eventually. "Safety" becomes table stakes, not a moat. At that point, Anthropic is competing on model quality alone in a market where compute and talent costs are enormous. The $100B Amazon commitment is not free — it locks Anthropic into AWS infrastructure in ways that may constrain strategic optionality. If I were on the board, I'd be asking hard questions about what Anthropic owns that can't be replicated by a well-funded competitor in 5 years.
