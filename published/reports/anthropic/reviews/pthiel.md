## The Verdict Upfront

Let me start with the contrarian question I always ask: **what important truth about Anthropic do very few people agree with?** 

Here is mine: Anthropic is not a 0→1 company. It is a very well-executed 1→n company that built a better version of OpenAI's product and captured enterprise market share by convincing regulated industries that safety is commercially valuable. That is admirable. It is also not the same thing as creating something genuinely new.

The secret Anthropic's founders had in 2021 was: "safety-first AI can be commercially viable in regulated industries." That secret was true. They proved it. But that's a market insight, not a technological paradigm shift. The underlying technology — transformer scaling — is the same paradigm as OpenAI. Constitutional AI is a training methodology, not a new paradigm.

This doesn't make Anthropic a bad company. It makes it a very good company in a very competitive market, without the kind of moat I'd need to feel confident it holds value at $965B.

## The Core Question I'm Not Seeing Answered

What is Anthropic's last-mover advantage? Who will Anthropic be when the AI market has consolidated in 10 years?

If the answer is "the company with the most safety research credibility," that's a meaningful but potentially fragile advantage — it depends on the continued perception that safety is a genuine differentiator, which requires continuous validation.

If the answer is "the company with the strongest enterprise relationships in regulated industries," that's a real moat but it's a distribution moat, not a technology moat.

If the answer is "the company that trains the best models," then Anthropic is competing in a market where the inputs (compute and talent) are both increasingly commoditized and expensive, and where Google, Microsoft, and Meta have structural advantages Anthropic can never match.

---

## On the Three-Cloud Strategy

This is the one decision I find most strategically puzzling. By distributing Claude on AWS, GCP, and Azure with no exclusivity, Anthropic ensures that Claude becomes a commodity layer that plugs into whoever else wins the cloud war. This maximizes short-term distribution and enterprise adoption. It also ensures that Anthropic never becomes the platform — it becomes the model API that serves whoever becomes the platform. Amazon and Google are happy with this arrangement. Are Anthropic's shareholders?

---

## Dissent Memo

My lower scores don't reflect disrespect for what Anthropic has built — it's a remarkable commercial achievement. They reflect the reality that at a $965B valuation, the question is not "is this a good company?" but "is this a company with durable monopoly economics?" I'm not convinced the answer is yes. The moats feel real but soft — the kind that a well-capitalized competitor with 3-4 years could meaningfully erode.
