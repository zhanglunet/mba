## ARR quality note (gross vs net)

The $47B is an annualized **run-rate**, officially disclosed mid-May with the Series H — but it is partly **gross**. Per Sacra, Anthropic recognizes cloud-reseller (AWS/GCP/Azure marketplace) **end-customer spend on a gross basis** — full customer spend booked as revenue, partner payouts as expense — inflating the top line vs net-reporting peers ([futuresearch](https://futuresearch.ai/anthropic-financial-forecast/); [sacra](https://sacra.com/c/anthropic/)). **Magnitude is contested:** a forced net restatement in IPO diligence could cut headline ARR **20–40% in one print** (bear case); FutureSearch counters that the reseller is the genuine customer-of-record (like Stripe/Shopify booking gross) and puts real auditor risk in **single digits**. A harsher skeptic view sits in *"Anthropic's Profitability Swindle"* ([wheresyoured.at](https://www.wheresyoured.at/anthropics-profitability-swindle/)). **Why it matters for a VC judge:** the $47B / 47x signal is real but its *cleanliness* is the single biggest tail risk on the revenue line — a ~21x-ARR public comp is highly sensitive to whether that ARR survives audit on a net basis. Score the signal, but discount the headline relative to net-basis competitors.
