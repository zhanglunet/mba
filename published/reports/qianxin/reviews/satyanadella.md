## Verdict

QiAnXin is making the right strategic moves: from compliance vendor to AI security platform, from government-only to broader enterprise, from point products to integrated XDR. These are exactly the transitions Microsoft has been navigating in security over the past decade. The gaps are real but addressable: brand identity outside government, platform coherence (are these truly integrated or just co-sold?), and compute infrastructure for AI training. The brand influence question — does QiAnXin's influence extend beyond its current customer base? — has a clear answer right now: no. But the trajectory is right.

---

## Critical Gap

The question I'd push hardest on: **Is QiAnXin a platform or a product collection?** Microsoft security was a product collection — Defender for this, Sentinel for that, Purview for another thing — until we consciously integrated them into a coherent platform with a unified data model, shared APIs, and a common AI layer (Copilot for Security). That integration is what allows us to claim "one platform for security" and have it mean something. **QiAnXin has Tianqing (endpoint), Tianyuan (threat intelligence), Wangshen (network) — but is there a unified data model? A shared security graph? A common API layer that third parties can build on?** If the answer is yes, they need to communicate it better. If the answer is no, that's the most important engineering investment they should be making right now. Platform integration is what separates a security vendor from a security platform — and only platforms get pricing power and ecosystem lock-in.

---

## Brand Action（90 天）

1. **Launch "QiAnXin Security Graph" as a developer API**: Following Microsoft's Graph API model, create a unified data interface that exposes QiAnXin's threat intelligence to developers and MSSPs. Build the ecosystem first, then charge for access. Ecosystem = brand moat.
2. **Partner with a global MSSP (managed security service provider) for a non-China market proof of concept**: A publicly referenceable customer in Singapore, UAE, or Europe demonstrates that QiAnXin's platform can operate outside the Chinese regulatory ecosystem — critical for international brand credibility.
3. **Rename products for the AI era**: "Tianqing AISOC" and "Tianyuan AI Intelligence" bridge the existing product heritage with the AI-first narrative. This is cheap signaling, but brand coherence signals organizational intent to customers and partners.
