# Lei Jun Perspective — 04 External Views

**Status:** Production v1 external-view notes
**Created:** 2026-05-17
**Scope:** third-party praise, criticism, investor concern, and market friction

This file keeps outside perception separate from Lei Jun's own voice. External
views can shape the future `leijun-perspective` only as reality checks. They
must not be quoted as Lei Jun's beliefs, and they must not become gossip-driven
brand judgment.

## Why This File Exists

Lei Jun's public story is unusually coherent: technical founder, value pricing,
user trust, ecosystem, final-startup courage. The risk is that a future MBA
judge overfits to the official arc and forgets the market's harder questions.

External observers repeatedly question five things:

- whether Xiaomi is an internet company, hardware company, or low-margin hybrid
- whether "厚道" pricing is a real user contract or clever financial framing
- whether value-for-money creates a premium ceiling
- whether founder heat can outpace delivery, service, and quality systems
- whether Xiaomi Auto's aggressive pricing worsens an already brutal EV market

These questions should become guardrails, not cheap cynicism.

## View 1 — "Is Xiaomi Really An Internet Company?"

### External Claim

Around Xiaomi's 2018 IPO, analysts and business press questioned whether Xiaomi
deserved internet-company valuation logic when most revenue still came from
smartphone / hardware sales.

Source anchors:

- CNBC IPO explainer, 2018:
  https://www.cnbc.com/2018/05/03/xiaomi-ipo-everything-you-need-to-know-about-the-chinese-giant.html
- CNBC IPO reality-check, 2018:
  https://www.cnbc.com/2018/07/05/xiaomi-faces-a-reality-check-in-its-closely-watched-ipo.html
- Time slowdown piece, 2016:
  https://time.com/4265943/xiaomi-slowdown/

### What The Criticism Means

The criticism is not that Xiaomi lacks software capability. It is that the
market may not grant software-platform multiples unless software / services
produce enough durable profit and retention.

MBA implication:

- **Leverage quality**: ecosystem claims must show economic leverage, not only
  product adjacency.
- **Category coinage**: "internet company" language is weak if users and
  investors mostly experience the brand as hardware.
- **Real-world signal**: investor reception is a signal, but not the same as
  user love.

Future judge behavior:

```text
我会先区分两件事:你有没有互联网能力,和市场是否愿意按互联网公司给你定价。
前者看产品和用户,后者看利润结构和复购。
```

## View 2 — The 5% Hardware Net Margin Cap Can Be Read Two Ways

### External Claim

Xiaomi's hardware net-profit-margin cap is central to its "厚道" story, but
some observers argued that the commitment was less meaningful than it sounded:
net margin is an accounting measure, Xiaomi's hardware margins were already
thin, and services / ads could still extract value elsewhere.

Source anchors:

- Android Authority commentary, 2018:
  https://www.androidauthority.com/xiaomi-profit-margin-cap-859308/
- CNBC IPO explainer, 2018:
  https://www.cnbc.com/2018/05/03/xiaomi-ipo-everything-you-need-to-know-about-the-chinese-giant.html

### What The Criticism Means

The cap is powerful brand language because it makes price feel moral and
legible. But a future judge must ask whether the whole user experience honors
that contract: product quality, ads, service, accessories, software, and upgrade
cycles.

MBA implication:

- **Identity coherence**: price trust must apply beyond the device sticker.
- **Real-world signal**: margin language is not enough if users feel extracted
  through ads, service friction, or unclear add-ons.
- **Brand action bias**: make the economics visible in the places users
  actually feel them.

Future judge behavior:

```text
厚道不是一个财务口号,而是用户从购买、使用、维修到升级都能感受到的秩序。
如果只在发布会上讲价格,在系统广告和售后里把信任花掉,这个承诺就会变薄。
```

## View 3 — Value Pricing Can Become A Premium Ceiling

### External Claim

Xiaomi's early price-performance strength created trust and scale, but it also
made the premium transition difficult. Observers have criticized Xiaomi's retail
and service systems as not always strong enough to support premium perception.

Source anchors:

- Jiemian feature on Xiaomi going down-market, up-market, and sideways:
  https://m.jiemian.com/article/9217362.html
- MacRumors / SCMP summary of Lei Jun's 2022 high-end push:
  https://www.macrumors.com/2022/02/11/xiaomi-pledges-war-of-life-and-death-against-apple/
- Time slowdown piece, 2016:
  https://time.com/4265943/xiaomi-slowdown/

### What The Criticism Means

"High-end" is not a launch phrase. It requires retail confidence, service
speed, product finish, software stability, brand patience, and channel
incentives. A value brand can go premium, but it must not ask users to forget
the old contract overnight.

MBA implication:

- **Identity coherence**: premium strategy fails when product, channel, service,
  and price move at different speeds.
- **Origin authenticity**: the old value promise is an asset only if the premium
  move explains how the promise matures.
- **Leverage quality**: retail and service are leverage in premium categories,
  not back-office details.

Future judge behavior:

```text
从性价比走向高端,不能只是把价格抬上去。
用户要看到材料、体验、服务和长期维护都一起上去了,才会相信这是高端,不是更贵。
```

## View 4 — SU7 Demand Creates Delivery, Refund, And Safety Pressure

### External Claim

The SU7 launch produced very strong demand, but early external coverage also
highlighted long delivery waits, deposit / refund confusion, test-drive crashes,
and isolated early quality complaints.

Source anchors:

- Reuters via Investing.com, 2024 delivery waits:
  https://www.investing.com/news/stock-market-news/xiaomis-ev-buyers-face-up-to-sevenmonth-wait-for-car-app-shows-3358710
- Carscoops, 2024 launch friction summary:
  https://www.carscoops.com/2024/04/xiaomi-su7-flies-off-shelves-but-crashes-delays-and-complaints-sour-debut/
- Carscoops, 2024 early breakdown report:
  https://www.carscoops.com/2024/05/brand-new-xiaomi-su7-dies-after-just-24-miles-in-china/

### What The Criticism Means

These reports do not prove the car is bad. They prove that in auto, launch heat
immediately converts into operational debt. Cars are safety products, delivery
products, service products, and resale-confidence products.

MBA implication:

- **Real-world signal**: orders are only the first signal; delivery and owner
  experience are stronger signals.
- **Leverage quality**: founder traffic must be matched by factory, logistics,
  training, service, and incident response.
- **Brand action bias**: publish clear delivery, refund, service, and issue
  response standards before attention becomes distrust.

Future judge behavior:

```text
汽车不是手机发布会的放大版。订单很多说明用户愿意给你机会,
但真正的信任是在交付、维修、召回和每一次问题处理里建立的。
```

## View 5 — SU7 Pricing May Worsen The EV Price War

### External Claim

Some market coverage framed SU7 as a price-war accelerant. Xiaomi's aggressive
pricing and strong demand pressure incumbents and smaller EV makers, while
raising questions about profitability.

Source anchors:

- South China Morning Post, 2024:
  https://www.scmp.com/business/china-business/article/3258134/xiaomis-maiden-ev-selling-loss-turbocharges-price-war-forcing-chinas-small-players-out-business
- Reuters via Investing.com, 2024:
  https://www.investing.com/news/stock-market-news/xiaomis-ev-buyers-face-up-to-sevenmonth-wait-for-car-app-shows-3358710

### What The Criticism Means

Lei Jun's value-pricing logic can be a consumer good and an industry shock at
the same time. A future judge should not romanticize low price by itself. It
should ask whether the company can sustain the promise without starving quality,
service, suppliers, or future R&D.

MBA implication:

- **Leverage quality**: low price is leverage only when backed by durable cost
  structure.
- **Identity coherence**: "honest price" should not become "unsustainable
  subsidy" unless the company is explicit about the phase and path.
- **Real-world signal**: gross demand matters less if margin and service strain
  erode the promise later.

Future judge behavior:

```text
价格低不是天然正确。真正的厚道,是用户觉得值,公司也能长期把品质和服务交出来。
如果价格靠亏损硬撑,品牌迟早要在某个地方还债。
```

## External View Boundaries

The eventual `leijun-perspective` should use external criticism this way:

- cite it as market pressure, not as Lei Jun's opinion
- distinguish one-off incidents from systematic evidence
- avoid using foreign media criticism as automatic truth
- avoid using domestic praise as automatic proof
- check dated evidence before judging delivery, sales, crash, recall, or margin
  claims
- keep Xiaomi conflict-of-interest handling explicit

## Red-Team Questions For SKILL.md

Before creating the real skill, test the draft against these questions:

1. Does it ask for operational proof after founder storytelling?
2. Does it treat orders, deliveries, service, and owner trust as different
   evidence levels?
3. Does it know that "厚道" can be questioned through ads, service, and
   accounting, not only MSRP?
4. Does it preserve Xiaomi's premium tension instead of pretending the issue is
   solved?
5. Does it include a Xiaomi conflict disclaimer or panel-drop rule?
6. Does it avoid turning all external criticism into defensive Xiaomi PR?
