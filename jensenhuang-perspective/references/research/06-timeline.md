fused into a single chronology with verified anchors. Every line below has

## A. 1963 — Birth and Early Childhood

- **1963-02-17**: Jen-Hsun Huang born in Tainan, Taiwan. Father Huang
  Hsing-tai, chemical engineer at an oil refinery. Mother Lo Tsai-hsiu,
  schoolteacher. Native speakers of Taiwanese Hokkien.
  - Anchor: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jensen_Huang
  - Anchor: https://www.britannica.com/money/Jensen-Huang
  - Anchor: https://computerhistory.org/profile/jensen-huang/
- **c. 1968**: At age 5, family moved to Thailand for his father's
  refinery career; lived there ~4 years.
  - Anchor: Wikipedia bio cross-anchored to Tae Kim, *The Nvidia Way*
    (https://wwnorton.com/books/the-nvidia-way).

## B. 1972–1981 — Emigration, Oneida Kentucky, Aloha Oregon

- **c. 1972 (age 9)**: Sent with his older brother to the United States
  by his parents, in response to his father's resolution to give them
  American educations.
  - Anchor: Wikipedia; cross-anchored to Stripe Sessions 2024 (Jensen
    notes "I came to the US in 1973 when I was 9", https://stripe.com/sessions/2024/a-conversation-with-nvidias-jensen-huang).
- **1973–1975**: Lived with relatives in Tacoma, Washington area;
  enrolled at Oneida Baptist Institute in Kentucky — a religious reform
  school for "difficult" children that his uncle had mistakenly thought
  was a prestigious boarding school.
  - Anchor: Wikipedia (citing Tae Kim's book).
  - Anchor: https://press.farm/a-biography-of-jensen-huang/
  - Detail: cleaned dormitory bathrooms; was bullied by older students,
    once nearly thrown off a footbridge with missing planks. Verified at
    https://www.cnbc.com/2024/03/15/nvidia-ceo-huang-at-stanford-pain-and-suffering-breeds-success.html.
- **c. 1976**: Family fully emigrated to the United States and reunited
  in Oregon.
- **c. 1978 (age 15)**: Worked as dishwasher at a Denny's restaurant for
  three summers running.
  - Anchor: https://stripe.com/sessions/2024/a-conversation-with-nvidias-jensen-huang
  - Anchor: https://www.gsb.stanford.edu/insights/jensen-huang-how-use-first-principles-thinking-drive-decisions
- **1981**: Graduated from Aloha High School, Aloha, Oregon.
  - Anchor: Computer History Museum profile,
    https://computerhistory.org/profile/jensen-huang/

## C. 1981–1984 — Oregon State University

- **1981–1984**: BS in Electrical Engineering, Oregon State University.
  Graduated with highest honors in 1984.
  - Anchor: https://engineering.oregonstate.edu/alumni-partners/oregon-stater-awards/searchable-awards-database/jen-hsun-huang-engineering-hall
  - Anchor: https://engineering.oregonstate.edu/all-stories/doing-important-hard-work-you-love
- **1981 (freshman year)**: Lab partner in electrical-engineering
  fundamentals was Lori Mills. They later married in 1985 (some sources
  say 1988 — the Tae Kim biography and Oregon State materials anchor
  the early-1985 wedding; press accounts vary).
  - Anchor: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jensen_Huang
  - Anchor: Tae Kim, *The Nvidia Way*.
  - Anchor: https://eboona.com/ai-startup-founder/jensen-huang/

## D. 1984–1993 — Early Career

- **1984**: Joined AMD as a microprocessor designer.
  - Anchor: https://nvidianews.nvidia.com/bios/jensen-huang (NVIDIA's
    own bio, cross-anchored by https://ethw.org/Jen-Hsun_Huang).
- **c. 1985**: Moved to LSI Logic; rose to become director of CoreWare
  (the division that built customer-specific chips for hardware vendors).
  - Anchor: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jensen_Huang
- **1985–1988**: Daughter Madison and son Spencer born.
  - Anchor: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jensen_Huang
- **1992**: Completed Master of Science in Electrical Engineering at
  Stanford University, while continuing to work full-time and attend
  evening classes.
  - Anchor: https://engineering.stanford.edu/about/history/heroes/2018-heroes/jensen-huang
  - Anchor: https://computerhistory.org/profile/jensen-huang/
- **1992 (Denny's plan)**: Jensen, Chris Malachowsky (Sun Microsystems
  engineer) and Curtis Priem (formerly IBM, Sun graphics designer) began
  meeting at a Denny's roadside diner on Berryessa Road in East San
  Jose to formulate a business plan.
  - Anchor: https://www.finance-monthly.com/jensen-huang-nvidia-dennys-origin-story/
  - Anchor: https://www.cnbc.com/amp/2024/05/04/jensen-huang-started-nvidia-at-a-dennys-breakfast-booth.html

## E. 1993 — Founding

- **1993-04-05**: Jensen Huang personally signed NVIDIA's original
  articles of incorporation. NVIDIA founded by Huang, Malachowsky and
  Priem. Each founder contributed $200; total initial capital ~$600.
  Filing handled by James Gaither of Cooley Godward.
  - Anchor: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nvidia
  - Anchor: NVIDIA corporate timeline,
    https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/about-nvidia/corporate-timeline/
  - Anchor: https://sequoiacap.com/podcast/crucible-moments-nvidia/
- **1993 (mid-year)**: Don Valentine at Sequoia Capital invested ~$1M.
  Sutter Hill Ventures invested ~$1M alongside. Post-investment valuation
  ~$6M. Valentine had been called by Jensen's former boss Wilf Corrigan
  at LSI Logic, who reportedly said "I have no idea what he just said.
  That was one of the worst pitches I've ever heard. But Wilf says to
  give you money."
  - Anchor: https://sequoiacap.com/podcast/crucible-moments-nvidia/
  - Anchor: https://x.com/bpoppenheimer/status/1785070305104351671
  - Anchor: Tae Kim, *The Nvidia Way*.

## F. 1994–1997 — Near-Death Era

- **1995-05**: NV1, NVIDIA's first chip, launched as a "multimedia card"
  with quadratic texture mapping and audio integration. Sega Saturn
  controller ports.
  - Anchor: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NV1
- **1995–1996**: NV1 sold ~250,000 units, mostly returned. After
  Microsoft announced DirectX would exclusively use triangle primitives,
  NV1's quadratic-texture approach was off-trend. Sega contracted NVIDIA
  to design NV2 for an upcoming console; that contract too went sideways.
- **1996**: NVIDIA cut headcount from ~100 to <40. Sega's CEO agreed
  to release NVIDIA from the NV2 contract while paying enough to keep
  the company alive.
  - Anchor: https://www.inc.com/peter-cohan/against-a-wall-how-jensen-huang-saved-nvidia-in-the-1990s/91065579
  - Anchor: NTU 2023 speech, https://blogs.nvidia.com/blog/huang-ntu-commencement/
- **1997-04**: RIVA 128 (NV3) launched, NVIDIA's first DirectX-compatible
  triangle-primitive chip.
  - Anchor: https://www.computer.org/publications/tech-news/chasing-pixels/famous-graphics-chips-nvidias-riva128
- **1997-08**: RIVA 128 shipped at retail. Within four months sold >1M
  units. NVIDIA had been at roughly 30 days' payroll when it shipped.
  - Anchor: Tae Kim, *The Nvidia Way*.
  - Anchor: https://www.inc.com/peter-cohan/against-a-wall-how-jensen-huang-saved-nvidia-in-the-1990s/91065579

## G. 1998–2005 — Becoming The GPU Company

- **1998**: RIVA TNT released; cemented NVIDIA's reputation in PC gaming.
  - Anchor: https://www.tomshardware.com/picturestory/715-history-of-nvidia-gpus.html
- **1999-01**: NVIDIA announced its initial public offering of 3.5M
  shares of common stock.
  - Anchor: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nvidia
- **1999-08-31**: GeForce 256 announced as "the world's first 'GPU'",
  combining hardware Transform & Lighting on a single chip.
  - Anchor: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GeForce_256
  - Anchor: NVIDIA corporate timeline,
    https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/about-nvidia/corporate-timeline/
- **1999-10-11**: GeForce 256 released.
- **2000**: NVIDIA acquired 3dfx Interactive.
  - Anchor: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nvidia
- **2000s**: GeForce 2 → 3 → 4 → FX → 6 → 7 → 8 series. Console wins
  with Xbox (original) graphics chip.

## H. 2006–2012 — The CUDA Decade

- **2006-11**: CUDA launched alongside the GeForce 8800 GTX (G80).
  CUDA forced programmable-compute into every NVIDIA GPU, doubling die
  area and cost.
  - Anchor: https://worldfinancialreview.com/jensen-huang-three-strategic-bets-that-has-propelled-nvidia/
  - Anchor: https://wccftech.com/nvidia-ceo-reveals-how-the-company-survived-an-existential-threat-after-forcing-cuda-on-gaming-gpus/
- **2007**: Internal Jensen decision (per NTU 2023 speech) to put CUDA
  into all NVIDIA GPUs going forward, despite negative gross-margin
  implications for years.
  - Anchor: NTU 2023, https://blogs.nvidia.com/blog/huang-ntu-commencement/
- **2009–2012**: Tegra mobile SoCs launched for phones, tablets, gaming
  devices. NVIDIA pursued the mobile graphics market.
- **2012-09**: AlexNet (Krizhevsky, Sutskever, Hinton) demonstrated deep
  learning on CUDA-enabled GeForce GPUs. The AI era began as a real
  data-point.
  - Anchor: external — widely cited in Stephen Witt, *The Thinking
    Machine* (https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/757558/the-thinking-machine-by-stephen-witt/).

## I. 2013–2016 — AI Pivot

- **2013**: Inducted to Oregon State Engineering Hall of Fame.
  - Anchor: https://engineering.oregonstate.edu/alumni-partners/oregon-stater-awards/searchable-awards-database/jen-hsun-huang-engineering-hall

- **2016-08**: Jensen personally hand-delivered the first DGX-1 to

  three times" self-label.
  - Anchor: 03-expression-dna A1.

## R. Naming Conventions
