## Case 1 — Kingsoft Pangu: Failure As Method Source

### Context

In the 2022 annual speech, Lei Jun revisits the Pangu software failure and
frames it as a serious low point rather than a small product miss. The point is
not "failure made me stronger" in generic form; the point is that a major
product failure forced him to understand management, product-market mismatch,
and personal resilience.

### Decision / Action

- Continue through the low point rather than narrating it away.
- Turn the failure into a later method source.
- Use public speech to make failure part of founder credibility.

### Cost

- Team morale collapse / personal pressure.
- Public admission that effort and ambition did not guarantee product success.

### What This Reveals

Lei Jun respects product proof more than founder effort. A team can be sincere,
hard-working, and still wrong. This is crucial for brand judging: he should not
give high scores just because a founder tells a painful origin story.

### MBA Judge Implication

- **Origin authenticity**: pain helps only if it leads to better decisions.
- **Real-world signal**: users do not pay for your hardship; they pay for the
  product that emerges after hardship.
- **Brand action bias**: when a brand is stuck, he will likely ask for a concrete
  product correction, not a better motivational story.

## Case 2 — Joyo.com Sale To Amazon: Timing And Capital Constraint

### Context

Joyo.com was an early Chinese e-commerce company founded by Lei Jun and Chen
Nian. In 2004, Amazon acquired it. Contemporary Sina coverage says Lei Jun
described the sale as a difficult but constrained choice; the company needed
capital and the market structure was shifting.

Source anchor:

- Sina 2004: `雷军首度详述出售卓越内幕：缺钱只能选择卖`.
  URL: https://tech.sina.com.cn/i/2004-11-26/1219465606.shtml

### Decision / Action

- Sell Joyo to Amazon rather than continuing an undercapitalized fight.
- Treat a painful exit as adaptation to market structure, not pure failure.

### Cost

- Loss of independent e-commerce upside.
- Later observers can argue the sale was too early / too cheap.
- Lei Jun's founder identity absorbs the lesson: timing and capital can beat
  passion.

### What This Reveals

Lei Jun's "顺势而为" is not passive. It means recognizing when the wave, capital
requirements, and competitive structure exceed what the current organization can
carry. This should keep the future SKILL from becoming naive perseverance talk.

### MBA Judge Implication

- **Leverage quality**: a brand's leverage must match the capital structure of
  the category.
- **Real-world signal**: being early is not enough if the company cannot fund
  the category's next phase.
- **Brand action bias**: he may recommend narrowing, partnering, or exiting
  rather than always "doubling down".

## Case 3 — Xiaomi Founding: Category Design From Three Capabilities

### Context

Xiaomi was not framed as a pure phone company. Lei Jun's later speeches and book
material repeatedly describe Xiaomi as a combined model: software, hardware,
internet / services, and later AI / ecosystem. The important decision is not
"make a phone"; it is "build a new consumer electronics company with internet
methods."

### Decision / Action

- Enter hardware through phone, but use software / internet iteration and fan
  participation as leverage.
- Make founder public communication part of the brand machine.
- Use price / value trust as an entry contract.

### Cost

- "Cheap" perception risk.
- Hardware supply chain and quality risk.
- Brand identity tension: enthusiast internet company vs mass consumer hardware
  company.

### What This Reveals

Lei Jun likes category designs that combine capabilities across old boundaries.
The category claim must be understandable to users, not only investors.

### MBA Judge Implication

- **Category coinage**: reward category language that explains a real user
  experience shift.
- **Identity coherence**: punish brands whose software, hardware, price, and
  service promises point in different directions.
- **Leverage quality**: community / founder IP is leverage only if product and
  supply chain can carry it.

## Case 4 — IPO Pricing / Hardware Net Margin Cap: Price As Trust Contract

### Context

The 2021 annual speech links Xiaomi's IPO pricing to Xiaomi's product pricing
ethic: "厚道" becomes a principle that can apply beyond phone MSRP. Xiaomi also
made a public commitment around hardware net profit margin, turning pricing into
an explicit brand contract.

Source anchors:

- 2021 annual speech transcript: https://finance.sina.com.cn/tech/2021-08-10/doc-ikqcfncc2102383.shtml
- China Entrepreneur / Sina reporting around Xiaomi hardware net margin cap
  (cross-check needed before final SKILL)

### Decision / Action

- Price IPO conservatively by analogy to product pricing.
- Publicly bind the brand to hardware-margin restraint.
- Use pricing morality to differentiate Xiaomi from opaque premium pricing.

### Cost

- Lower near-term extraction.
- Harder premium positioning later.
- Market may interpret "厚道" as "cheap" rather than "trustworthy value".

### What This Reveals

Lei Jun sees price as relationship design. The brand says: if you trust us with
your money, we will not abuse information asymmetry.

### MBA Judge Implication

- **Identity coherence**: price, product quality, and message must agree.
- **Real-world signal**: user trust is stronger when the brand's economics are
  legible.
- **Failure mode**: a brand can trap itself if users only remember the low-price
  half of the promise.

## Case 5 — Premium / High-End Push: Escaping The "Cheap" Lock-In

### Context

Xiaomi's high-end strategy asks whether a brand built on value density can also
earn premium trust. This is one of Lei Jun's most important brand tensions:
"价格厚道" can open the door, but it can also become a ceiling.

### Decision / Action

- Push Xiaomi phones and ecosystem products into higher-end territory.
- Invest in technology, design, retail, and brand language that can support
  premium perception.
- Keep user-value language while raising aspiration.

### Cost

- Old users may fear Xiaomi is abandoning value.
- Premium users may still see Xiaomi as not premium enough.
- The brand must hold two ideas at once: high-end and honest.

### What This Reveals

Lei Jun's brand logic is not "be cheap forever"; it is "make premium feel worth
it and still not betray trust." That tension is central to how he should judge
other brands.

### MBA Judge Implication

- **Identity coherence**: premium transition only works if product, service,
  design, and price all mature together.
- **Category coinage**: high-end language without product proof is punished.
- **Brand action bias**: prove premium with hard product / service evidence,
  not only celebrity launch language.

## Case 6 — Enter Smart EVs: Research Before Courage

### Context

In 2021, Xiaomi formally announced entry into smart EVs. Multiple reports and
Lei Jun's own later annual speech describe the process: a U.S. sanction scare,
board-level strategic question, 70+ / 75 days of investigation, many industry
interviews, management meetings, board meetings, and then a public commitment.

Source anchors:

- Sina / launch transcript: https://finance.sina.com.cn/tech/2021-03-30/doc-ikknscsk5048514.shtml
- Economic Observer: https://www.eeo.com.cn/2021/0331/483388.shtml
- 2024 annual speech mirror: https://www.digitaling.com/articles/1241022.html

### Decision / Action

- Enter the smart EV category.
- Lei Jun personally leads.
- Xiaomi commits long-term capital and reputation.
- Declines easy narrative of "internet company disrupts cars" in favor of
  research, seriousness, and long cycle.

### Cost

- Massive capital requirement.
- Brand risk if product fails.
- Founder reputation becomes exposed collateral.
- Category has safety, manufacturing, delivery, and service consequences much
  heavier than phones.

### What This Reveals

Lei Jun's "courage" is not impulse. It is an end state after investigation. This
is the most important heuristic for auto-panel judging.

### MBA Judge Implication

- **Leverage quality**: reward preparation before public commitment.
- **Origin authenticity**: the origin story is credible when it includes fear,
  investigation, and cost.
- **Real-world signal**: auto brands need proof across testing, delivery,
  service, and user trust.

## Case 7 — Full-Stack Auto / No Shortcut: Respect The Industry

### Context

The 2024 annual speech and SU7 launch emphasize that Xiaomi did not want to
simply buy a shell, outsource the hard parts, or shortcut core technology. Lei
Jun frames the auto move as "守正" before "出奇": first respect the industry,
then differentiate.

### Decision / Action

- Build core capabilities rather than relying only on contract manufacturing or
  branding.
- Invest in testing, R&D, factory, supply chain, and ecosystem integration.
- Position SU7 as part of `人车家全生态`.

### Cost

- Slower path to market.
- Larger up-front investment.
- More operational exposure.

### What This Reveals

Lei Jun believes category entry requires humility toward old constraints. A new
entrant's story is only credible if it shows respect for what incumbents already
know.

### MBA Judge Implication

- **Leverage quality**: punish brands that confuse software speed with hardware
  reliability.
- **Identity coherence**: ecosystem claims must reduce user friction.
- **Brand action bias**: before telling a bigger story, prove the unglamorous
  basics.

## Case 8 — AI / OS / Chip Direction: Long-Term R&D As Brand Proof

### Context

The 2023 annual speech upgrades Xiaomi's technology strategy around
`(software x hardware)^AI`. Public material also points toward OS, chip, and
AI investment as long-horizon proof that Xiaomi is not just assembling devices.

### Decision / Action

- Frame AI as an exponent over software-hardware integration.
- Invest in system-level and chip-level capability.
- Use R&D spend / technical roadmap as part of brand proof.

### Cost

- Long payback cycle.
- Harder public communication: users see product now, not infrastructure.
- Risk of overclaim if technical proof lags.

### What This Reveals

Lei Jun treats technology investment as a brand asset when it is tied to user
experience. R&D is not backstage; it helps users believe the ecosystem will keep
improving.

### MBA Judge Implication

- **Category coinage**: AI language must map to actual product behavior.
- **Real-world signal**: technical demos matter only when they improve user
  trust or daily experience.
- **Brand action bias**: show the user-facing consequence of hard tech.

## Emerging Decision Heuristics

These are candidates for the future SKILL.md:

1. **Courage starts after research**: do not praise boldness unless the team can
   show how it investigated the category.
2. **Price is a relationship**: every price decision teaches users whether the
   brand is trustworthy.
3. **Founder IP must carry risk, not just traffic**: the founder's face matters
   only when attached to operational responsibility.
4. **Respect old constraints before claiming new category power**: especially in
   hardware, auto, education, and other high-consequence categories.
5. **Premium requires proof across the whole experience**: design, service,
   quality, and language must mature together.
6. **R&D becomes brand when users can feel it**: invisible technology spend only
   matters when it changes trust, experience, or future confidence.
7. **Know when to sell / narrow / partner**: not every hard category is solved by
   perseverance; capital structure and timing are real.
