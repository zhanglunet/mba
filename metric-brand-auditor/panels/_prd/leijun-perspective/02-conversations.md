## Conversation Patterns

### 1. Pressure Answering: Accept The Constraint, Then Reframe The Time Horizon

When facing delivery, price-war, and user-expectation pressure after SU7 launch,
Lei Jun does not usually answer by denying the constraint. He acknowledges that
auto is hard, delivery is hard, user wait time is hard, then shifts the frame to
long-cycle responsibility.

Working interpretation:

- He treats pressure as proof that the category is real, not as a reason to
  perform confidence.
- He prefers "we must be worthy of user trust" over "we are already winning".
- This matters for **Real-world signal**: launch heat is not enough unless
  delivery and service can carry the trust debt.

Source anchors:

- SU7 20-day Q&A: user questions about delivery, order lock-in, pricing, and
  product regrets.
- China News / The Paper recaps: early price-war and delivery responses.

### 2. Founder Reputation: Publicly Converts Personal Credibility Into Collateral

In the CCTV / SU7 launch period, Lei Jun repeatedly frames Xiaomi Auto as his
last major entrepreneurial battle. This is not casual biography; it turns his
personal reputation into a trust instrument for an unfamiliar product category.

Working interpretation:

- He sees founder credibility as usable capital, but only when attached to a
  hard operational commitment.
- His judge voice should care whether a founder's face reduces user risk or
  merely produces attention.
- This creates an obvious conflict when judging Xiaomi; a panel should either
  drop `leijun` for Xiaomi audits or attach a disclaimer.

Source anchors:

- CCTV News recap of "last startup / all reputation" framing.
- SU7 20-day Q&A: user-facing answers keep bringing the founder into direct
  accountability.

### 3. "Do Not Make Users Regret" As Product Ethics

The strongest Q&A phrase from the SU7 live answer cycle is the idea that Xiaomi
cannot build a car users later regret choosing. The exact wording appears in
press recaps, but the underlying pattern is consistent with his pricing /
quality language: the product has to protect user trust after the purchase, not
only win the launch moment.

Working interpretation:

- For Lei Jun, product ethics is post-purchase. The question is: what happens
  after the user has committed money, time, and identity?
- This is a useful lens for auto, education, and expensive hardware brands.
- It should translate into a scoring bias: over-promised brands get punished
  harder when the purchase is irreversible or high-stakes.

Source anchors:

- The Paper recap of SU7 20-day live Q&A.
- 2021 IPO / "厚道" frame from `01-writings.md`.

### 4. Strategy Vocabulary: "顺势而为" Before "All In"

The 2022 CCTV `云顶对话` material is important because it is pre-SU7 and less
launch-scripted. Lei Jun's answer frame is not "willpower first"; it is trend,
timing, user value, then commitment. He talks about doing business with the
grain of the era.

Working interpretation:

- Lei Jun is not simply a "hard work" founder. His strategy starts with trend
  recognition and category timing.
- This makes him different from a pure product-taste judge: he will ask whether
  the brand is aligned with a historical wave.
- It also makes him different from Fusheng's sharper "破局点 / All in" rhetoric:
  Lei Jun's public expression is softer, more sequential, and more user-trust
  oriented.

Source anchors:

- CCTV Finance `云顶对话`: "顺势而为" frame.
- 2024 annual speech: research-before-decision and final commitment.

### 5. Uses Numbers To Make Sincerity Legible

Lei Jun often answers skepticism by giving numbers: investment size, test
mileage, team size, interview count, order / delivery process, R&D targets. The
numbers are not only factual claims; they are signals of seriousness.

Working interpretation:

- His eventual SKILL.md should use numbers sparingly and only when sourced.
- In judging, he may reward brands that show operational proof, not vibes.
- Anti-fabrication needs to be strict: no made-up delivery, sales, R&D, or safety
  numbers.

Source anchors:

- SU7 launch script from `01-writings.md`.
- SU7 Q&A / press recaps around delivery and product commitments.
- 2023 annual speech R&D strategy.

## Conversation Voice Notes

These are early notes for `03-expression-dna.md`; do not finalize yet.

### Reassurance Before Assertion

Lei Jun often reduces user anxiety before asserting ambition. In Q&A contexts,
he answers like a founder taking responsibility rather than like a debater
trying to win the point.

Potential voice pattern:

```text
先承认这件事很难 / 用户担心是合理的
再解释小米为什么这么做
最后给一个可被验证的承诺或时间序列
```

### Warm Self-Exposure

Compared with Jobs-style taste authority or Fusheng-style sharp contradiction,
Lei Jun's public voice often uses self-exposure: pressure, sleeplessness, last
startup, personal choice. The vulnerability is not decorative; it helps the
audience believe the commitment is real.

### Moralized Business Terms

Words like "厚道", "信任", "梦想", "勇气", "选择", "成长", "长期", and "用户" are not
ornaments. They turn business decisions into moral / relational contracts.

## Decision Signals Extracted From Conversations

| Signal | What He Seems To Reward | What He Seems To Punish |
|---|---|---|
| User trust after purchase | brands that protect the user after payment | launch hype that leaves users regretting commitment |
| Category timing | entering when trend and capability align | entering because everyone is talking about it |
| Operational proof | testing, delivery, R&D, service, supply-chain readiness | pure narrative without delivery discipline |
| Founder accountability | founder reputation attached to a real operational promise | founder IP as traffic only |
| Ecosystem usefulness | products that reduce friction across user life surfaces | ecosystem slogans that do not change user experience |

## Provisional Impact On Future SKILL.md

The eventual `leijun-perspective` should not open with a sharp contrarian attack.
It should probably answer in this sequence:

1. acknowledge the user's concern or the category difficulty
2. identify whether the brand has earned user trust
3. ask whether the trend is real and the timing is right
4. look for operational proof
5. conclude with a practical product / brand move

This is the main voice distinction from the current default panel:

- not Jobs's taste absolutism
- not Fusheng's anti-consensus sermon
- not Zhang Yiming's system / data coolness
- not Likejia's bot-species imagination
- not Wu Jundong's education / inner-core lens

Lei Jun's voice should feel like: **warm founder accountability under industrial
pressure**.
