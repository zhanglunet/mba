## What harness farm actually is

**Public footprint: approximately zero as a CBC brand.**

The English noun "harness" carries both the user-cited senses — (a) the literal yoke/挽具 fitted on an animal, and (b) the figurative *harness*=利用/驾驭. In the 2026 AI-agent discourse, sense (b) clearly dominates: "Harness" is shorthand for the *runtime / scaffolding / containment shell* around an LLM that lets agents run unattended for hours ([36kr explainer](https://www.36kr.com/p/3736640995721472); [Harness Engineering 知乎 deep-dive](https://zhuanlan.zhihu.com/p/2021716359613032366)). It is the same metaphor as Anthropic's internal "agentic harness" usage — software that *drives* the model, not animal tack.

So if "harness farm" is a CBC term, the most plausible reading is **"a farm of harnesses" — a portfolio/factory of agent-runtime scaffolds**, not a stable of yoked animals. The "farm" suffix evokes server-farm / GPU-farm / model-farm — i.e., a fleet of harness configurations being run/tested in parallel. This would slot naturally as a *transitional infrastructure layer* between an early-stage program (ai坊) and a productized atelier (智坊): generate harnesses → graduate the survivors.

But again: **zero public source confirms this is a CBC product.** The headline Harness players in Chinese press (李笛 / 明日新程, 海致科技) are CBC-orthogonal ([量子位](https://www.qbitai.com/2026/04/401065.html); [36kr 海致 piece](https://36kr.com/p/3789107286432777)).
