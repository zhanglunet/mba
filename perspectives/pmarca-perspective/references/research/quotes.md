## Market is the only thing that matters (product/market fit)
- "market is the most important factor in a startup’s success or failure" — *Startups Part 4, "The only thing that matters"*, June 25, 2007 — https://pmarchive.com/guide_to_startups_part4.html
- "In a great market—a market with lots of real potential customers—the market pulls product out of the startup." — same
- "The #1 company-killer is lack of market." (Rachleff’s Law) — same
- "When a great team meets a lousy market, market wins." — same
- "When a lousy team meets a great market, market wins." — same
- "When a great team meets a great market, something special happens." — same
- "The only thing that matters is getting to product/market fit." (Rachleff’s Corollary) — same
- "Product/market fit means being in a good market with a product that can satisfy that market." — same
- "You can always feel when product/market fit isn’t happening." — same
- "And you can always feel product/market fit when it’s happening." — same
- "Markets that don’t exist don’t care how smart you are." — same
- "Product quality and market size are completely different." — same
- "People are terrible at understanding causation." — same

## The onion theory of risk (fundraising)
- "let me introduce the onion theory of risk" — *Startups Part 2, "When the VCs say ’no’"*, June 20, 2007 — https://pmarchive.com/guide_to_startups_part2.html
- "the best thing you can do to optimize your chances of raising money is to take out risk." — same
- "Peel away at the onion." — same
- "There is something wrong with your plan." (when many VCs say no) — same
- "Never, ever say that you have no competitors." — same
- "Great markets draw competitors, and so if you really have no competition, you must not be in a great market." — same
- "The most valuable thing you can do is actually build your product. When in doubt, focus on that." — same
- "VCs work mostly through referrals" — *Startups Part 3*, June 25, 2007 — https://pmarchive.com/guide_to_startups_part3.html
- "any individual VC can only fund a few companies per year" — same

## Big companies = Moby Dick
- "The most important thing you need to know going into any discussion or interaction with a big company is that you’re Captain Ahab, and the big company is Moby Dick." — *Startups Part 5, "The Moby Dick theory of big companies"*, June 27, 2007 — https://pmarchive.com/guide_to_startups_part5.html
- "The behavior of any big company is largely inexplicable when viewed from the outside." — same
- "don’t do startups that require deals with big companies to make them successful." — same
- "never assume that a deal with a big company is closed until the ink hits the paper" — same
- "don’t get obsessed. Don’t turn into Captain Ahab." — same
