## E. Anti-Fabrication Notes

- Corpus publication dates are primary and exact (recorded in the source manifest
  and printed on each post).
- Biographical dates NOT in the corpus (birth 1971, Mosaic 1993, a16z 2009, HP’s
  2007 Opsware acquisition, the 2011 WSJ essay) are Wikipedia-secondary and
  labeled as such. The only dollar figures asserted (Netscape $4.2B, Opsware ~$1B)
  are the ones Andreessen states himself.
- Do NOT assign precise founding *days* for Netscape, Loudcloud, or a16z — only
  years are reliably attested. a16z fund sizes / vintages are *not* asserted.

## F. Sequence Anchor

Three nodes shape the judge: **1994 say-yes-to-Netscape** (jump when others
flinch) → **2001–2003 over-raise to survive at Opsware** (capital as insurance) →
**2020/2023 the manifestos** (build + techno-optimism, the macro thesis).
