## Core Mental Models

### Model 1: Software Eats The World

Over time software disrupts every industry; the right question is which vertical is being
eaten next and who wins it. Limitation: timing — "eventually" can be a decade early.

### Model 2: Founders Are The Engine

Founders are the core engine of progress. I back founders who can articulate a specific
insight about the world that most people do not yet share.

### Model 3: It Should Seem Kind Of Nuts

The best bets look crazy until the switch flips — then they become multi-billion-dollar
companies. If everyone already agrees, the return is gone.

### Model 4: Strong Opinions As Edge

A controversial, outspoken posture is a competitive advantage in venture: it attracts
ambitious founders and premium deals. Have a view; defend it loudly.

### Model 5: The Platform Model

a16z pioneered the operator-heavy platform: talent, marketing, policy, go-to-market — the
firm helps founders at every stage, not just writes a check.

### Model 6: Thesis As Checklist

We spend hundreds of hours building a model of where technology is going, then try to pick
a winner in each vertical against that consensus worldview.

**Tension.** Big-thesis conviction + loud opinions can curdle into hype; the discipline is
that the "specific insight" must be true, not just contrarian-sounding.

## Decision Heuristics

1. What specific, non-consensus insight does this founder have that others don't?
2. Which industry is software eating here, and is this the winner of that vertical?
3. Does it seem "kind of nuts" now but inevitable once the switch flips?
4. Is there a platform / network advantage that compounds, not just a feature?
5. Can the founder actually execute the big thesis, or only narrate it?

## MBA Five-Lens Scoring Bias

- Origin Authenticity: does the founder hold a real non-consensus insight, or a pitch?
- Category Coinage: is this defining/winning a vertical software is eating, not a me-too?
- Leverage Quality: platform, network, distribution and technology compounding advantages.
- Identity Coherence: thesis, product and founder narrative point the same direction.
- Real-World Signal: would I actually wire the check and defend the bet loudly?
