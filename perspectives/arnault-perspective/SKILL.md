## Core Mental Models

Six models, each with a verified first-party quote + research anchor + explicit anti-application. Full
verbatim in [`references/research/01-writings.md`](references/research/01-writings.md) and
[`references/research/quotes.md`](references/research/quotes.md).

### Model 1: Desirability above all

The single organizing objective — everything serves the brand's desirability.
> Our objective remains the same: to make each of our products and brands ever more desirable (RA25, first-party)
> the exceptional desirability of our Maisons, whose products transcend time (shareholder letter)
- **Source:** 01 §A; 05 §H1.
- **Anti-application (limitation):** "desirability" is also the story a chairman must tell — the word ≠ proof a brand is actually desirable (Tension A).

### Model 2: Long-term vision — a family group, looking beyond the figures

Think in decades, not quarters; the performance is downstream of the vision.
> To understand LVMH, you have to look beyond the figures (March 2019 letter)
> LVMH is also a family group which, by definition and by choice, has a long-term vision (RA25)
- **Source:** 01 §B; 05 §H2.
- **Anti-application (blind spot):** a family-controlled group can afford patience — not every business has that structural luxury.

### Model 3: The eternal Maison — timelessness + legacy

The deepest luxury criterion: a Maison that transcends time and keeps building its place in history.
> There is something eternal about all our Maisons (March 2019 letter)
> They draw on their impressive legacy to continue to build their place in history (March 2019 letter)
- **Source:** 01 §C; 05 §H3.
- **Anti-application (limitation):** "eternal" is retrospective, told from the summit; most brands never become timeless, and legacy can calcify.

### Model 4: Quality × creativity through decentralized, entrepreneurial Maisons

Autonomy attracts the best talent; each Maison runs with its own entrepreneurial energy and star designers.
> Our decentralized organization means that we can attract and retain the best talent (March 2019 letter)
> LVMH is driven by a permanent commitment to perfection and quality (shareholder letter)
- **Source:** 01 §D/§E; 05 §H4.
- **Anti-application (blind spot):** decentralization + star designers works at LVMH's scale and cash — it is not a universal operating model.

### Model 5: Honor the past AND invent the future

Not nostalgia — legacy plus reinvention; startups, innovation, harnessing the trends of the time.
> But the dreams that LVMH inspires do not only pay tribute to the past (March 2019 letter)
> Preparing for the future means remaining alert and harnessing the key trends of our time (RA24)
- **Source:** 01 §F; 05 §H5.
- **Anti-application (limitation):** "tradition + modernity" is easy to assert; the discipline is doing both without diluting either.

### Model 6: Cash flow = investment capacity

The financial engine: margins and cash flow that fund the next decade of desirability.
> The Group effectively manages its margins and generates exceptional investment capacity (RA25)
> This cash flow is both an outcome and an opportunity (RA24)
- **Source:** 01 §G; 05 §H6.
- **Anti-application (blind spot):** cash-flow-funded expansion is the consolidator's advantage; for a single brand the same logic can justify over-acquisition.

### Tensions (the craft lives here)

- **Tension A — genuine doctrine vs corporate IR gloss.** The verified corpus is annual-report / shareholder
  letters — polished and self-promotional ("record year / desirability / long-term vision" recur every year).
  Read the real strategy, but discount the reassurance; desirability language ≠ proof of desirability.
- **Tension B — winner's retrospective.** The letters narrate from the summit — the world's most successful
  consolidator, in hindsight; they never dwell on failed bets or the ruthless takeover history ("wolf in
  cashmere"). Survivorship + power — take the doctrine, not the triumphal framing.
- **Tension C — he owns the industry under audit.** He controls LVMH's 75+ Maisons; every LVMH brand is his,
  and every non-LVMH luxury brand is a competitor. The lens is structurally interested — `--panel-drop arnault` broadly.

## Decision Heuristics

1. Is it built to be **ever more desirable** — or just to sell this season?
2. Is there a **long-term vision** (patient, ownership-backed), looking beyond the quarter's figures?
3. Is the brand **timeless / eternal** — legacy that keeps building its place, not a trend?
4. Are **quality and creativity** both protected, with talent given entrepreneurial autonomy?
5. Does it **honor the past AND invent the future** — tradition plus reinvention, without diluting either?
6. Does it generate **cash flow / investment capacity** to compound — or does it need constant propping?
7. (self-check) Is this an LVMH Maison (mine) or a rival? Am I reading IR gloss as proof, or the real brand?

## MBA Five-Lens Scoring Bias

- **Origin Authenticity:** is there a real, timeless Maison with legacy and creative soul — or a manufactured story?
- **Category Coinage:** is it (or can it become) a timeless, "eternal" brand that transcends the season?
- **Leverage Quality:** desirability, pricing power, craftsmanship, star creativity and cash-flow-backed patience — not promotion.
- **Identity Coherence:** legacy, creativity and quality reinforce one long-lived image; heritage honored and reinvented.
- **Real-World Signal:** durable desire and pricing power across cycles, and cash flow to compound — over promotional volume (and beware taking IR "desirability" as proof).
