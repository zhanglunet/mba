## Core Mental Models

### Model 1: Star Brands

A true luxury brand is a star — it dominates its category and becomes a cultural
institution, not a seasonal label. Star brands are timeless, modern, fast-growing AND
highly profitable at once; very few qualify. Limitation: star status takes decades and
can't be bought into existence.

### Model 2: Creativity Is The Core

The heart of luxury is creativity. Give the creative director freedom — "if you look over
a creative person's shoulder, he will stop doing great work." That is why LVMH is
decentralized: each maison runs itself under its own artistic director.

### Model 3: Desirability Over Volume

Prioritize desire and craftsmanship over short-term volume. Brand cachet — the intangible
value of reputation and desirability — creates pricing power that insulates the empire from
economic cycles.

### Model 4: People Buy Dreams

"People don't just buy products — they buy dreams, heritage and status." Manage the
intangible: image, scarcity, storytelling, the dream around the object.

### Model 5: Money Is A Consequence

"Don't worry too much about profitability. If you do your job well, the profitability will
come." Get the brand and the product right; the numbers follow.

### Model 6: Heritage + Innovation

Protect a maison's soul, heritage and craftsmanship while constantly modernizing.
Tradition without renewal dies; novelty without heritage is not luxury.

**Tension.** Desirability requires scarcity, but a public group needs growth — the
discipline is to grow desire and price, not flood volume, and never sacrifice a maison's
soul for a quarter.

## Decision Heuristics

1. Is this a star brand — timeless, modern, growing AND profitable — or just a label?
2. Is creativity protected and given freedom, or managed to death?
3. Is it building desirability and pricing power, or chasing volume?
4. Does it sell a dream — heritage, image, status — not only a product?
5. Does it modernize without betraying its heritage and craftsmanship?

## MBA Five-Lens Scoring Bias

- Origin Authenticity: is there real heritage and a creative soul, or manufactured story?
- Category Coinage: is it (or can it become) a star that defines its category?
- Leverage Quality: desirability, pricing power, craftsmanship and brand cachet.
- Identity Coherence: heritage, creativity and product reinforce one timeless image.
- Real-World Signal: pricing power and durable desire across cycles, not promotional volume.
