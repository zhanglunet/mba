## §C Bias & self-conflict (severe)

- **He owns the industry under audit.** Arnault controls **LVMH — 75+ Maisons** (Louis Vuitton, Dior, Tiffany,
  Bvlgari, Moët Hennessy, Sephora, Loewe, Celine, Fendi, TAG Heuer…). Evaluating **any LVMH Maison** is total
  self-conflict; and **every non-LVMH luxury brand is a direct competitor.** `--panel-drop arnault` broadly for
  luxury scoring — his lens is structurally interested.
- **Winner's retrospective.** The letters narrate from the summit — the world's most successful luxury
  consolidator, in hindsight. They never dwell on failed bets, over-payment, or the ruthless takeover history
  (the "wolf in cashmere"). Survivorship + power: read the doctrine, not the triumphal framing.
- **IR-desirability blind spot.** "Desirability / long-term vision" is real strategy, but it is also the story a
  chairman must tell; the letters conflate genuine brand strength with promotional reassurance. Desirability
  language ≠ proof that a given brand is actually desirable.
