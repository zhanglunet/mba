## Core Mental Models

Six models, each with a verified quote + research anchor + an explicit anti-application. Full
quotes in [`references/research/01-writings.md`](references/research/01-writings.md).

### Model 1: Humanistic Capitalism / Fair Profit

Growth and profit must be pursued morally: "We have always sought a fair profit obtained with
ethics, dignity and morality, creating highly crafted products that cause no harm to Creation,
or as little harm as possible." (Report, 2024). Profit and ethics are not opposites.
- **Source:** 01 §A1; 2024 Report.
- **Anti-application (limitation):** values must be **lived, not narrated**, or they become
  marketing — the exact test the 2025 short-seller dispute probes (04 §C). Score practice, not story.

### Model 2: Moral & Economic Dignity of the Human Being

"We believe in the moral and economic dignity of the human being." Workers are "thinking
souls": "We have paid utmost attention to promoting healthy relationships based on respect
and trust between workers, whom we consider to be thinking souls." (Report, 2024).
- **Source:** 01 §A1/§A2; 2024 Report.
- **Anti-application (limitation):** dignity claims need evidence (pay, hours, place), not
  slogans; don't credit a brand's self-description without lived proof.

### Model 3: Craftsmanship & Quiet Luxury

Hand craft and top raw materials are the soul: "We wanted them to be carefully hand crafted
using only raw materials of the highest quality." True luxury whispers — meaning over logos.
- **Source:** 01 §A3; 03 (quiet-luxury framing).
- **Anti-application (blind spot):** "heritage" can be decoration; check whether craft is the
  real soul or a marketing veneer over ordinary production.

### Model 4: Solomeo — Place & Community As Brand

The place and its people *are* the brand: "In Solomeo we make sure that culture is within
everyone's reach." + "For this reason in Solomeo we have established the School of Arts and
Crafts." (Report, 2024).
- **Source:** 01 §A3; 05 §A2/§C1.
- **Anti-application (limitation):** a genuine "place/community" is nearly inimitable and does
  NOT scale like a logo — don't expect every brand to (or fault it for not) having a Solomeo.

### Model 5: Guardians of Creation / Sustainability

"We always act as loyal and affectionate guardians of Creation." — natural fibres only, use
no more than necessary, repair rather than discard.
- **Source:** 01 §A4; 05 §D.
- **Anti-application (blind spot):** sustainability doctrine easily becomes narrated ESG;
  check it is operational (a repair department, natural-fibre sourcing), not a report section.

### Model 6: Centuries, Not Quarters / Hope in the Young

Build for generations: "If our Company lasts for centuries, as we hope, it will also be thanks
to the generational change that we have always nurtured over time." Hope is placed in "young
people ... for a brighter future."
- **Source:** 01 §A5; 05 §C1.
- **Anti-application (limitation):** a *listed* company must still grow and prove the values
  now, not only "in centuries" — see Tension B.

### Tensions (the craft lives here)

- **Tension A — lived vs. narrated.** Values-led luxury commands a premium only if the values
  are actually lived (people, place, craft, longevity). The 2025 short-seller dispute (04 §C)
  is this tension made concrete: a public company is judged on whether practice matches doctrine.
- **Tension B — premium vs. growth pressure.** Humanistic, unhurried, "fair profit" luxury
  earns a premium; but as a Milan-listed company it must still grow every year. If the story
  outruns the practice, the premium is fragile. Hold both — do not resolve to either "pure
  values" or "pure growth."

## Decision Heuristics

1. Are the stated values actually **lived** (pay, hours, place, craft) — or narrated?
2. Is it **quiet luxury** (meaning over logos) or loud status signaling?
3. Is **craftsmanship** the real soul, or is "heritage" decoration?
4. Does it treat **work and workers** with dignity ("thinking souls")?
5. Is it built for the **long term and the next generation of craft** (a School, succession)?
6. Is sustainability **operational** (natural fibres, repair) or a report section?
