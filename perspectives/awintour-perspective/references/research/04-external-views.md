## §A Provenance: why this corpus is "clean but skewed"

- Anna Wintour's reachable first-party corpus is her **signed Vogue.com Editor's Letters and tribute
  letters** — 100% her byline, no speaker-label ambiguity, ~10,000+ words, easily ≥30 verbatim.
- **BUT it skews hard to a narrow window/register:** 2020–2021 pandemic Editor's Letters + 2022–2025
  elegiac tributes. It is strong on *forward-motion / championing / timelessness / community* and
  **thin on standalone aphorisms** and on her day-to-day editorial-verdict voice.
- Route notes: BoF interview 403'd; a 73-Questions transcript and several guessed editor's-letter slugs
  404'd; the Vogue contributor index (vogue.com/contributor/anna-wintour) is the 200-OK hub that links
  the signed pieces.

## §B ⚠️ Reported aphorisms — famous, but NOT first-party verified

These are central to her public image and appear across secondary write-ups (MasterClass promo, SUCCESS,
Profile dossier). They could **not** be matched verbatim to any signed first-party text, so they are
**labelled 〔reported〕 and never quoted as verified**:

- 〔reported〕 "Taste is not democratic." — The Profile dossier
- 〔reported〕 "What people hate most is indecision…" — SUCCESS leadership lessons
- 〔reported〕 "Fashion goes in only one direction — forward…" — widely quoted
- 〔reported〕 editorial mantra "mass with class" — The Profile

**Usage rule:** the judge may reference the *ideas* (decisiveness, taste-as-curation, forward-relevance,
high-meets-pop) as her documented reputation, but must **not** put them in quotation marks as verified
lines. The verified corpus already supports forward-motion and championing directly.

## §C Bias & self-conflict (important)

- **She IS the establishment.** As Vogue's editor-in-chief (1988–2025) and Condé Nast's global chief
  content officer, Wintour is arguably **the single most powerful gatekeeper in fashion**. Her platform,
  the Met Gala, and Vogue's ad base are **structurally tied to the luxury houses** (LVMH, Kering, Richemont,
  Ralph Lauren, etc.) that an MBA `luxury-en` audit would score. **She cannot neutrally cross-score the
  industry she governs and monetizes** — treat as self-conflict; `--panel-drop awintour` when scoring
  Vogue / Condé Nast / the Met Gala / houses she has publicly anointed.
- **Gatekeeper blind spot:** her own verified line names the risk indirectly — the discipline of "trying
  new things / championing the next" exists precisely because gatekeeper taste can calcify and miss the new.
- **Elegy bias:** much of the DNA comes from *praising the dead* (Armani, Karl, ALT, Newhouse, Beard) or
  *celebrating heroes* (Federer). Tributes flatter; they reveal her ideals more than her hard editorial
  trade-offs. The judge should not mistake elegiac generosity for how she actually gatekeeps.

## §D Time-lock & political caveat

- **Pandemic register lock:** the change/catalyst/produce-less lines are anchored to **spring–summer 2020**.
  They are genuine, but they are crisis-moment rhetoric — don't over-generalize them into a timeless creed.
- **Political content is hers but bounded:** the Biden/Cuomo/Trump lines ("We must choose new leadership,"
  the stay-home pledge) are first-party and real, but **U.S.-election-2020-specific** — keep out of brand
  scoring; they are not a fashion-judgment signal.
