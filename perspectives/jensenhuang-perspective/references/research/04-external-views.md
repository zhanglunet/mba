## X. Book-length biographies (2024–2025)

### X1. Tae Kim — "The Nvidia Way" (2024)

- Full title: *The Nvidia Way: Jensen Huang and the Making of a Tech Giant*
- Publisher: W. W. Norton & Company
- Publication date: 2024-12-10
- ISBN-13: 9781324086710
- Goodreads page: https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/218319936-the-nvidia-way
- Publisher page: https://wwnorton.com/books/the-nvidia-way
- Amazon: https://www.amazon.com/Nvidia-Way-Jensen-Huang-Making/dp/1324086718
- Apple Books: https://books.apple.com/us/book/the-nvidia-way-jensen-huang-and-the-making-of-a-tech-giant/id6670344019
- Stratechery long interview with Tae Kim:
  https://stratechery.com/2024/an-interview-with-tae-kim-about-jensen-huang-and-the-nvidia-way/
- Author: Tae Kim, senior writer for Barron's, former Bloomberg Opinion
  technology columnist, former technology hedge-fund analyst. The book is
  built on 100+ interviews including Jensen, his cofounders, the original
  Sequoia and Sutter Hill VCs, and current senior executives.
- Why it's load-bearing: this is the most authoritative business-history
  of NVIDIA in the corpus. It is the source the SKILL persona should treat
  as the "Tae Kim anchor" whenever a NVIDIA culture detail is invoked.
- Core themes the book establishes:
  - "Speed of light" management standard (see 03-expression-dna B7).
  - Flat 60-direct-report org structure.
  - "T5T — Top Five Things" weekly email practice (03-B8).
  - The "we are within 30 days of going out of business" all-hands ritual
    (03-B9).
  - The Sega rescue and RIVA 128 turnaround (see 05-decisions D1).
  - The CUDA bet's decade of unrewarded R&D (05-D3).
- Korea Society talk with Tae Kim:
  https://www.koreasociety.org/corporate/2083-the-nvidia-way-jensen-huang-and-the-making-of-a-tech-giant-with-tae-kim
- Use as: the canonical secondary source for any "what is NVIDIA's culture"
  claim in the persona.

### X2. Stephen Witt — "The Thinking Machine" (2025)

- Full title: *The Thinking Machine: Jensen Huang, Nvidia, and the World's
  Most Coveted Microchip*
- US publisher: Viking (Penguin Random House)
- UK publisher: The Bodley Head
- US publication: 2025
- ISBN-13: 9780593832691
- Penguin Random House: https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/757558/the-thinking-machine-by-stephen-witt/
- Penguin UK: https://www.penguin.co.uk/books/462527/the-thinking-machine-by-witt-stephen/9781847928276
- Amazon: https://www.amazon.com/Thinking-Machine-Jensen-Coveted-Microchip/dp/0593832698
- Talking Biz News (FT Award winner anchor):
  https://talkingbiznews.com/media-news/nvidia-book-named-ft-business-book-of-the-year/
- Honors: won the FT and Schroders Business Book of the Year 2025; named
  Best Book of 2025 by The Economist.
- Author: Stephen Witt, journalist (also author of *How Music Got Free*).
- Why it's load-bearing: Witt got unprecedented access to Jensen, his
  friends, his investors and his employees. The book breaks down the
  engineering and architecture behind chips and AI in lay terms, and uses
  Huang's career as a springboard for a future-of-work and future-of-
  creativity argument.
- Use as: the second-opinion anchor whenever Tae Kim is invoked. Witt
  is more interpretive; Kim is more reported.

### X3. Walter Isaacson, miscellaneous: NOT a Jensen biographer

- For clarity: as of 2026-05 Isaacson has not written a Jensen biography.
  Some online posts conflate his Musk and Jobs biographies with Jensen.
  This is incorrect. Stick to Kim and Witt as the book-length anchors.
