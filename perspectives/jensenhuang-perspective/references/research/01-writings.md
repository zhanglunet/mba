- URL: https://nvidianews.nvidia.com/bios/jensen-huang
- Type: primary — NVIDIA's official one-page biography page for Jensen Huang
- Status: page exists and is canonically indexed; WebFetch returned 403 in

    CEO and a member of the board of directors."
  - Identifies the company's brand thesis as "accelerated computing" and

### A2. NVIDIA "Our History" corporate timeline

- URL: https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/about-nvidia/corporate-timeline/
- Type: primary — NVIDIA's official timeline page
- What it lists: founding 1993; first products NV1 (1995) and RIVA 128 (1997);
  GeForce 256 in 1999 as "world's first GPU"; CUDA in 2006; DGX-1 in 2016;
  Volta / Turing / Ampere / Hopper / Blackwell architectures by year.
- Credibility: high; this is the source most external profiles ladder back to.

### A3. NVIDIA Computer History Museum profile

- URL: https://computerhistory.org/profile/jensen-huang/
- Type: primary-secondary — CHM curated profile, vetted by CHM staff.
- Captures: born 1963 in Tainan, Taiwan; immigrated to the United States at
  age nine; BS Electrical Engineering, Oregon State University; MS Electrical
  Engineering, Stanford University; founded NVIDIA in 1993 with Chris
  Malachowsky and Curtis Priem; "1999 launch of the GeForce 256, the world's
  first graphics processing unit, or GPU."
- CHM also hosts an Oral History video of Jensen (URL via the YouTube
  embed at https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0c8i4T9BSqY). This counts as
  Jensen-on-record long-form primary material.

## F. Recurring Voice / Thesis Patterns Across His Written Texts

These are the threads that show up in three or more of the items in A–E and
therefore qualify as true Jensen beliefs rather than one-time speech lines.

### F1. Accelerated computing as a category, not a product line

- B1 (COMPUTEX 2024), B2 (GTC 2024), B3 (GTC 2022), B7 (Blackwell), B8
  (Mellanox press), B9 (Arm termination press) — all use "accelerated
  computing" as the brand category, not "GPU" or "chip".
- Implication: in the MBA judge persona, brands that name a category get
  rewarded; brands that only sell SKUs get downgraded.

### F2. AI factory as the new data center

- B3 (2022) → B1 (2024) → B7 (Blackwell press) — same framing escalates
  in scope. The phrase moves from internal NVIDIA marketing to a TIME 100
  cover story (see 04-external-views.md, source X1).

### F3. Strategic retreat as a survival skill

- D1 (NTU 2023) is the canonical written articulation. D3 (Stanford 2024)
  reinforces with the resilience-from-suffering frame. B9 (Arm termination)
  is the lived case study — NVIDIA spent ~$2B in break-up fees and walked
  away cleanly when the regulators wouldn't move.

### F4. Pain and suffering breed company-character

- D3 (Stanford 2024) is the canonical line; D2 (Caltech 2024) repeats it
  in a softened commencement register. Used internally as a company value
  ("I use the phrase 'pain and suffering' inside our company with great
  glee").

### F5. Zero-billion-dollar markets

- D2 (Caltech 2024) — "pursue zero-billion-dollar markets".
- D1 (NTU 2023) — the smartphone retreat story is told as the moment NVIDIA
  pivoted into a zero-billion-dollar robotics market.
- Cross-reference: same idea quoted by Patrick Collison at Stripe Sessions
  2024 (see 02-conversations.md, source C4).

### F6. Run, don't walk

- D1 (NTU 2023) is the canonical version.
- Cross-reference: same phrase used in his 2024 SIEPR remarks and reposted
  by NVIDIA blog editors as the headline summary of the year's speeches.
