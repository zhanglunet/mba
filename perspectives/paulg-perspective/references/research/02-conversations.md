## A. Talks Graham Published as Essays (first-party spoken word, verbatim)

These are the cleanest conversational primary sources: PG gave the talk, then
posted his own text.

### A1. "How to Start a Startup" — March 2005 — Harvard Computer Society

- Text: https://www.paulgraham.com/start.html
- The talk that seeded Y Combinator (the Summer Founders Program grew out of the
  reaction to it — see `06-timeline.md`).
- Verbatim: "You need three things to create a successful startup: to start with
  good people, to make something customers actually want, and to spend as little
  money as possible."

### A2. "What You'll Wish You'd Known" — January 2005 — written for a high-school talk

- Text: https://www.paulgraham.com/hs.html
- The "stay upwind, keep your options open, work on hard problems" life-strategy
  voice, pitched to teenagers. Useful for how PG simplifies without dumbing down.

### A3. "Before the Startup" — October 2014 — Stanford CS183B, Lecture 3

- Text: https://www.paulgraham.com/before.html
- Part of Sam Altman's "How to Start a Startup" Stanford course (Fall 2014). The
  lecture series is on YouTube; PG's own transcript is the essay.
- Anchor line: "Startups are very counterintuitive." — the thesis of the whole
  talk: your instincts mislead you, so you need explicit, counterintuitive rules.

### A4. "Startup School" / YC talks (recurring, 2007→)

- YC's Startup School and "Office Hours" featured PG for years. Many are on
  YouTube (ycombinator channel). Treat individual video quotes as **requires
  live verification** unless matched to a PG-published transcript.

## D. Conversational Register vs. Essay Register (for the SKILL author)

| Trait | In essays | In talks / interviews |
|---|---|---|
| Sentence length | Medium, balanced, footnoted | Shorter; more "you" address |
| Contrarianism | Stated as a calm axiom | Delivered as a "this will sound crazy, but…" |
| Examples | Airbnb / Stripe / Wufoo, dated | Same examples, told as stories |
| Hedging | Precise ("usually," "in my experience") | Same; PG rarely overclaims live |

The talk voice (start.html, hs.html, before.html) is the same reasoning engine
as the essays, just with more direct second-person address ("you"). When the
MBA judge "speaks," the essay register is the safer default.
