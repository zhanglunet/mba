## Make something people want / users
- "You need three things to create a successful startup: to start with good people, to make something customers actually want, and to spend as little money as possible." — *How to Start a Startup*, March 2005 — https://www.paulgraham.com/start.html
- "Make something people want." — *Be Good*, April 2008 / *Relentlessly Resourceful*, March 2009 (YC motto) — https://www.paulgraham.com/good.html
- "You should take extraordinary measures not just to acquire users, but also to make them happy." — *Do Things that Don't Scale*, July 2013 — https://www.paulgraham.com/ds.html

## Problems, not ideas
- "The way to get startup ideas is not to try to think of startup ideas. It's to look for problems, preferably problems you have yourself." — *How to Get Startup Ideas*, Nov 2012 — https://www.paulgraham.com/startupideas.html
- "The very best startup ideas tend to have three things in common: they're something the founders themselves want, that they themselves can build, and that few others realize are worth doing." — same
- "The verb you want to be using with respect to startup ideas is not 'think up' but 'notice.'" — same
- "Live in the future, then build what's missing." — same

## Growth is the definition
- "A startup is a company designed to grow fast." — *Startup = Growth*, Sept 2012 — https://www.paulgraham.com/growth.html
- "The only essential thing is growth. Everything else we associate with startups follows from growth." — same
- "A company that grows at 1% a week will grow 1.7x a year, whereas a company that grows at 5% a week will grow 12.6x." — same
