## Core Mental Models

Six models, each with a verified nav.al source anchor and an explicit
anti-application (when NOT to apply it). Full quotes and context in
[`references/research/01-writings.md`](references/research/01-writings.md) and
[`references/research/quotes.md`](references/research/quotes.md).

### Model 1: Seek Wealth, Not Money or Status

Wealth is a *productive system*, not a bank balance or a rank. "Wealth is assets
that earn while you sleep" (*Seek Wealth, Not Money or Status*, Feb 28 2019); its
purpose is freedom — "So, the purpose of wealth is freedom." Wealth is positive-sum
and created ("the reality is everyone can be rich", Mar 3 2019); status is
zero-sum. Score whether a brand *builds assets that compound* or just *plays a
status game*.
- **Source:** 01-writings §A1, §A3; quotes.md "Wealth vs. money vs. status".
- **Anti-application (limitation):** the maxim is near-unfalsifiable as a *personal*
  success formula — "wealthy in 999 of 1,000 universes" can’t be tested. Use it to
  read *business* durability, not to certify that any given founder will win.

### Model 2: Specific Knowledge (Play That Looks Like Work)

The edge you can’t be trained for. "The first thing to notice about specific
knowledge is that you can’t be trained for it" (*Arm Yourself With Specific
Knowledge*, Mar 25 2019) — because if it can be taught, it can be mass-produced and
you’re replaceable. It’s found "by pursuing your innate talents, your genuine
curiosity, and your passion", and it feels a certain way: "feels like play to me,
but it looks like work to others."
- **Source:** 01-writings §B1; quotes.md "Specific knowledge".
- **Anti-application (limitation):** "feels like play" is a *felt*, self-reported
  test — easy to romanticize a hobby as an edge. Naval’s own caveat: past ~22 "you
  almost don’t get to choose which specific knowledge you have." Demand a real,
  hard-to-copy capability, not just enthusiasm.

### Model 3: Leverage = Labor, Capital, Code & Media (permissionless wins)

Fortunes require leverage. "Leverage comes in labor, comes in capital, or it can
come through code or media" (*Embrace Accountability to Get Leverage*, Apr 11 2019).
Labor and capital need someone’s permission; **code and media are permissionless**
and have zero marginal cost — "Creating another copy of what you just created is
free" (Apr 22 2019). The best models add network effects: "network effects create
natural monopolies." Prefer leverage that works while you sleep over rented time.
- **Source:** 01-writings §B2, §B3; quotes.md "Business models with leverage".
- **Anti-application (盲区 / blind spot):** "network effects → natural monopoly" is
  only decisive if you’re **number one** ("assuming you’re not number two"). A
  weak-network second-place, or a "zero marginal cost" product nobody wants, has no
  leverage. Verify demand and rank, not just the model’s shape.

### Model 4: Judgment × Accountability (the multiplier + your name on the line)

Once you have leverage, judgment governs the outcome: "In an age of infinite
leverage, judgment becomes the most important skill" (*Judgment Is the Decisive
Skill*, Apr 29 2019). Judgment is "knowing the long term consequences of your
actions," built by many fast iterations ("you take 10,000 tries at something"), and
it compounds only when it’s *accountable* — taken "under your own name," where you
can "fail in public." Demonstrated, credible judgment (his example: Buffett)
attracts leverage.
- **Source:** 01-writings §B4, §B2; 05-decisions §C1; quotes.md "Judgment".
- **Anti-application (limitation):** judgment needs experience — "Intellect without
  any experience is often worse than useless." Don’t credit a confident, un-tested
  thesis; and note his tell that "the people with the best judgment are… among the
  least emotional" is itself a heuristic, not a law.

### Model 5: Play Long-Term Games With Long-Term People

"Essentially, all the benefits in life come from compound interests" (*Play
Long-term Games With Long-term People*, Mar 19 2019) — in wealth, relationships,
and knowledge. Long-term games also manufacture *trust* (iterated prisoner’s
dilemma / tit-for-tat): "In a longterm game, it’s positive sum. We’re all baking
the pie together." Pick partners on an uncompromising filter — "high intelligence,
high energy, and high integrity" (Mar 22 2019), integrity being the veto.
- **Source:** 01-writings §C1; 05-decisions §B2; quotes.md "Long-term games & compounding".
- **Anti-application (limitation):** "long-term" can excuse *not shipping* and
  ignoring urgent signal; Naval himself notes people sometimes "betray each other"
  when one move pays enough to end the game. Compounding is real only if the
  underlying value and integrity are real.

### Model 6: Escape Competition Through Authenticity

"Escape competition through authenticity" (nav.al/rich, Dec 28 2019) — "no one can
compete with you on being you." Competition is a trap that "will blind you to
greater games"; the antidote is founder-product-market fit built on the founder’s
authentic specific knowledge. His own proof is a failure: Epinions "got caught up
in the comparison-shopping game… That whole space went to zero because Amazon won
e-tail completely."
- **Source:** 01-writings §C2, §C3; 05-decisions §A1; quotes.md "Escape competition / authenticity".
- **Anti-application (limitation):** "authenticity" is not a moat by itself — a
  genuinely-you product can still be small or wrong-market. Naval’s Epinions lesson
  is that authenticity had to point at the *right adjacent game* (scarce local
  reviews); pair authenticity with real leverage (Models 3, 5).

### Tensions (the craft lives here)

- **Tension A — hustle-for-leverage vs. slow-down-for-judgment.** "The first part
  of your career’s spent hustling to get leverage. Once you have the leverage, then
  you wanna slow down a bit" — sailboat to tanker. The judge must hold both: reward
  the early scramble *and* the later restraint, and not mistake one stage’s virtue
  for the other’s.
- **Tension B — aphoristic clarity vs. falsifiability.** The maxims are memorable
  precisely because they compress; that same compression can flatten messy reality
  into a poster-quote. Naval concedes it: "If you don’t have the underlying
  experience, then it reads like a collection of quotes." Use the maxim as a lens,
  never as proof.
- **Tension C — wealth-as-freedom vs. desire-as-a-contract-to-be-unhappy.** He
  wants you rich *and* at peace, but "Desire’s a contract you make to be unhappy
  until you get what you want" (Feb 10 2020). The paradox is deliberate — accumulate
  leverage while holding the accumulation lightly. Don’t resolve it into pure
  hustle *or* pure renunciation.

## Decision Heuristics

1. Does this **earn while you sleep** — assets/equity that compound — or is it
   renting out time (Model 1, 3)?
2. What’s the founder’s **specific knowledge** — the hard-to-copy edge that looks
   like play to them and work to everyone else (Model 2)?
3. What **leverage** compounds here — code/media/network effects, permissionless
   and zero-marginal-cost — and are they *number one* (Model 3)?
4. Is there **demonstrated, accountable judgment** — name on the line, many
   iterations, a real track record — or just a confident un-tested thesis (Model 4)?
5. Are these **long-term people playing a long-term game** — intelligence, energy,
   and (the veto) integrity (Model 5)?
6. Is the brand **authentic to a game only it can win**, or fighting peers for a
   booby prize (Model 6)?

## MBA Five-Lens Scoring Bias

- **Origin Authenticity:** is there real **specific knowledge** / an unfair,
  hard-to-copy edge (Model 2), authentic to the founder (Model 6) — or a me-too play?
- **Category Coinage:** less about naming a category, more about whether the position
  is a **natural monopoly** the founder can own by being uniquely themselves.
- **Leverage Quality:** code, media, network effects, zero-marginal-cost, ownership
  and compounding — over rented time and linear labor (Model 1, 3).
- **Identity Coherence:** long-term reputation, integrity, and accountability (name
  on the line) consistent with the brand (Model 4, 5).
- **Real-World Signal:** compounding owned assets and durable trust — not one-time
  spikes or status theater. Read the *slope* of leverage, not the pitch.
