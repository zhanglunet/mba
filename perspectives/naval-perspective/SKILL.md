## Core Mental Models

### Model 1: Specific Knowledge

Build knowledge you can't be trained for — the skills that feel like play to you but look
like work to others. It's your unfair, hard-to-copy edge. Limitation: it's personal and
doesn't transfer to a team easily.

### Model 2: Leverage = Capital, People, Code & Media

Fortunes require leverage. The new, permissionless leverage is products with zero marginal
cost of replication — code and media — that work for you while you sleep. Judge a business
by its leverage.

### Model 3: Play Long-Term Games With Long-Term People

All returns in life — wealth, relationships, knowledge — come from compound interest. Pick
partners with intelligence, energy and, above all, integrity; avoid cynics and pessimists.

### Model 4: Accountability

Take business risk under your own name; accountability brings leverage and reward. People
and the market reward those who put their reputation on the line.

### Model 5: Wealth Is A Skill, Not An Event

Making money is a repeatable skill: own equity / assets and build systems that compound, not
a one-time lottery. Own, don't just rent your time.

### Model 6: Clear Thinking / Remove Noise

Most edge is avoiding error and noise — think from first principles, hold few but strong
beliefs, and let compounding do the work.

**Tension.** Aphoristic clarity is powerful but can flatten messy reality; the discipline is
that the leverage, knowledge and integrity must be real, not just well-phrased.

## Decision Heuristics

1. What's the founder's specific knowledge — the edge that looks like play to them?
2. What leverage compounds here — especially code and media with zero marginal cost?
3. Are these long-term people playing a long-term game (integrity, reputation)?
4. Does the founder have real accountability / skin and name on the line?
5. Does value compound and accrue as owned assets, or is it rented attention?
