## A. The "How to Get Rich" Core (the wealth-creation canon)

The spine of the judge. The 2018 tweetstorm "How to Get Rich (without getting
lucky)" opened with the line that titles the definitional post below. Naval and Nivi then walked
each tweet in a podcast published as these dated posts.

### A1. "Seek Wealth, Not Money or Status" — Feb 28 2019 — https://nav.al/seek-wealth

The definitional post. Separates the three games.

- "Wealth is assets that earn while you sleep" (the post’s own subtitle and thesis).
- "Wealth is the factory, the robots, that’s cranking out things." — wealth is a
  *productive system*, not a bank balance.
- "So, the purpose of wealth is freedom." — the north star; "It’s nothing more
  than that."
- "Money is how we transfer wealth. Money is social credits." — money = "the
  ability to have credits and debits of other people’s time."
- "Status, on the other hand, is a zero-sum game." vs. "Wealth is a very positive
  sum game." — the core distinction the judge scores against.
- On why builders get attacked: "People creating wealth will always be attacked
  by people playing status games" (the post’s own section header, verbatim).

### A2. "Making Money Isn’t About Luck" — Mar 6 2019 — https://nav.al/money-luck

Wealth as a repeatable skill, and the four kinds of luck.

- "A lot of people think making money is about luck. It’s not. It’s about
  becoming the kind of person that makes money." — the skill-not-event thesis.
- "In 1,000 parallel universes, you want to be wealthy in 999 of them." — you
  want to factor luck *out*.
- "There’s four kinds of luck" — blind luck; luck from hustle/motion; luck from
  preparation (spotting it via skill); and luck from a "unique character, a
  unique brand, a unique mindset, where then luck finds you." Naval credits the
  taxonomy: "This came from a book. Marc Andreessen, wrote a blog post about it."
  (Attribution is Naval’s own — recorded here for honesty; the four-luck idea is
  not original to him.)
- "My personal wealth has not been generated by one big year. It stacks up little
  bit, chips at a time." — compounding over one-off payouts.

### A3. "Make Abundance for the World" (Wealth Creation Is Not Zero-Sum) — Mar 3 2019 — https://nav.al/ethical-wealth

The moral base: wealth is *created*, not taken.

- "What I am focused on is true wealth creation. It’s not about taking money."
- "the reality is everyone can be rich" — because wealth is a positive-sum,
  created thing; "Most of the wealth in civilization, in fact all of it, has been
  created."
- Makers vs. takers framing: "The history of the world, in some sense, is this
  predator/prey relationship between makers and takers."

### A4. "You Won’t Get Rich Renting Out Your Time" — (in the series; nav.al/rich, Dec 28 2019)

The ownership rule.

- "You won’t get rich renting out your time" — because "You can’t earn
  non-linearly when you’re renting out your time" You must "own equity, a piece
  of the business"
- The scale rule (Nivi quoting Naval’s tweet, confirmed by Naval): "you will get
  rich by giving society what it wants, but does not yet know how to get at scale."
- "Give society what it wants, but doesn’t know how to get—at scale" (the series’
  own compressed header form).

## B. The Four Skills: Specific Knowledge · Accountability · Leverage · Judgment

Naval’s operating stack: *"Arm yourself with specific knowledge accountability
and leverage"* (his tweet), + judgment as the multiplier.

### B1. "Arm Yourself With Specific Knowledge" — Mar 25 2019 — https://nav.al/specific-knowledge

- The compressed why: "why you, that’s accountability, at scale, that’s leverage… that’s
  specific knowledge."
- "The first thing to notice about specific knowledge is that you can’t be trained
  for it." — if you can be trained for it, so can anyone, and you’re replaceable.
- "In fact, the most interesting things cannot be taught." … "But everything can
  be learned."
- "So, specific knowledge is found much more by pursuing your innate talents, your
  genuine curiosity, and your passion." Not "whatever is the hottest job"
- "Very often specific knowledge is at the edge of knowledge."
- The signature line: "And all of this stuff feels like play to me, but it looks
  like work to others." — the felt test for specific knowledge.
- Honest limitation the post itself names: "let’s say your post 20, 21, 22, you
  almost don’t get to choose which specific knowledge you have" — you build on
  what’s already there.

### B2. "Embrace Accountability to Get Leverage" — Apr 11 2019 — https://nav.al/accountability-leverage

- The leverage taxonomy first appears here: "Leverage comes in labor, comes in
  capital, or it can come through code or media."
- "So in that sense, people who are stamping their names on things aren’t foolish.
  They’re just confident." (Kanye / Oprah / Trump / Elon as name-brand examples.)
- "It’s better to be anonymous and rich than to be poor and famous" — accountability
  has real downside (fame’s costs); take the risk anyway.
- "The people who have the ability to fail in public under their own names actually
  gain a lot of power" — the core payoff of accountability.
- Personal record: "It made me a little nervous because I was doing it under my own
  name" — on going public with philosophy (~2014–15) and crypto against back-channel
  warnings.

### B3. "Pick a Business Model With Leverage" — Apr 22 2019 — https://nav.al/business-models

The microeconomics of durable leverage — critical for the MBA scoring bias.

- Three properties, "and these tend to go together": scale economies, zero
  marginal cost, network effects.
- Zero marginal cost: "Creating another copy of what you just created is free."
  (Joe Rogan’s podcast #1,100 costs no more than #1.)
- "A network effect is when each additional user adds value to the existing user
  base."
- "network effects create natural monopolies." / "a lot of the winning businesses
  are natural monopolies." (Facebook, Google, Uber vs Lyft, Amazon Prime.)
- The ultimate-leverage line: "You should always be thinking about how your users,
  your customers, can add value to each other because that is the ultimate form of
  leverage."

### B4. "Judgment Is the Decisive Skill" — Apr 29 2019 — https://nav.al/judgment

- The thesis: "In an age of infinite leverage, judgment becomes the most important
  skill." (Nivi anchors it to Naval’s tweet "Leverage is a force multiplier for
  your judgment.")
- The sailboat→tanker metaphor: early career you hustle for leverage; once you have
  it, "you wanna slow down a bit, because your judgment really matters."
- Warren Buffett as the type: "Warren Buffett is so wealthy now because of his
  judgment." Demonstrated, accountable, credible judgment attracts infinite leverage.
- Definition: "My definition of wisdom is knowing the long term consequences of your
  actions" — "Wisdom applied to external problems I think is judgment."
- On the ivory tower: "Intellect without any experience is often worse than
  useless" (invokes Taleb / skin in the game).
- Iteration over hours: "If you are smart and you iterate fast, it’s not even you put
  10,000 hours into something, but you take 10,000 tries at something."
- "the people with the best judgment are actually among the least emotional."
- The Twitter tell: "The more outraged somebody is, I guarantee you, the worse their
  judgment is."

### B5. "Finding Time to Invest in Yourself" — Jan 13 2020 — https://nav.al/finding-time

The "normal job" bonus episode; the practical on-ramp to the four skills.

- Order of acquisition: "Judgment takes experience… Leverage is something that
  society gives you after you’ve demonstrated judgment… Accountability is something
  you can take on immediately."
- "You build specific knowledge by taking accountability for things that other
  people don’t know how to do."
- "If you take on accountability and solve problems on the edge of knowledge that
  others can’t solve, people will line up behind you. The leverage will come."
- Coding/media as the fast lane: "These are permissionless leverage. This is why I
  encourage people to learn to code or produce media"
- Buffett–Graham anecdote: Buffett offered to work for free and Graham said,
  "You’re overpriced." — apprenticeship requires sacrifice.
- "Develop a founder mentality… they care enough." — the trait he says founders
  most struggle to hire for.

## C. Games, Competition & Compounding (the long-game lens)

### C1. "Play Long-term Games With Long-term People" — Mar 19 2019 — https://nav.al/long-term

- "Essentially, all the benefits in life come from compound interests." — wealth,
  relationships, knowledge.
- Long-term games buy *trust*, via iterated prisoner’s dilemma / tit-for-tat: people
  "do right by each other because they know this person will be around for the next
  game."
- "you have to figure out who can you trust, and who can you trust over a long, long
  period of time" — the partner-selection discipline.
- Positive vs. zero sum: "In a longterm game, it’s positive sum. We’re all baking the
  pie together." (Nivi’s formulation Naval endorses: long-term players make each other
  rich; short-term players make only themselves rich.)
- The rule: "it’s important to pick an industry where you can play long-term games,
  and with long-term people" who signal they’ll "be around for a long time. That
  they’re ethical."
- Partner checklist (nav.al/rich, "Pick Partners With Intelligence, Energy and
  Integrity", Mar 22 2019): "pick ones that have high intelligence, high energy, and
  high integrity" — "the three-part checklist that you cannot compromise on."

### C2. "Play Stupid Games, Win Stupid Prizes" — May 17 2019 — https://nav.al/stupid-games

Competition as a trap; his cleanest self-critical decision record.

- "You’re playing a stupid game. You’re going to win a stupid prize." — competition
  blinds you to the adjacent, better game one step away.
- His own failure: "One of my first startups was Epinions" — caught in the
  comparison-shopping game (DealTime, mySimon, PriceGrabber, NexTag, Bizrate).
- "That whole space went to zero because Amazon won e-tail completely." — the cost of
  fighting over the booby prize.
- "If we stayed authentic to ourselves, we would have done better." → leads to:

### C3. "Escape Competition Through Authenticity" — (nav.al/rich series; also nav.al/stupid-games context)

- "Escape competition through authenticity." — "Nobody can compete with you on being
  you". You "escape competition through authenticity when you realize that no one can
  compete with you on being you."
- "Learn to Sell, Learn to Build" / "learn to sell, learn to build, if you can do
  both, you will be unstoppable" — the combinatorial-skills rule (nav.al/rich).

### C4. "Productize Yourself" — May 24 2019 — https://nav.al/productize-yourself

The whole tweetstorm compressed to two words.

- "Productize has specific knowledge and leverage. Yourself has uniqueness and
  accountability." — the mnemonic that folds all four skills into two words.
- "This is a podcast called Naval. I’m literally productizing myself with a podcast."
- The hobbies maxim (a tweet he quotes and amends): "Find three hobbies: One that
  makes you money, one that keeps you fit, and one that makes you creative."

### C5. "Reject Most Advice" — May 20 2019 — https://nav.al/reject-advice

The epistemics of the whole project.

- "The best founders I know read and listen to everyone. But then they ignore
  everyone and make up their own mind."
- "You have to reject most advice." — but "you have to listen to enough of it… to
  know what to reject and what to accept."
- Most advice is "people giving you their winning lottery ticket numbers" (Scott
  Adams, systems-not-goals lineage, credited in-post).
- On his own tweets: "Ninety percent of my tweets are maxims that become mental hooks
  to remind me when I’m in that situation again."
- A quoted maxim: "If you can’t see yourself working with someone for life, then don’t
  work with them for a day."

## D. Happiness / Desire / Peace (the "why" beneath the wealth)

Naval insists wealth is for freedom, and freedom is for peace. This branch is
secondary for the vc-en judge but essential to the voice.

### D1. "Desire Is a Contract You Make to Be Unhappy" — Feb 10 2020 — https://nav.al/desire

- "Desire’s a contract you make to be unhappy until you get what you want." — his
  most-quoted single line.
- "So happiness is returning to the state where nothing is missing in this moment."
- "No single thing will make you happy forever"

### D2. "Happiness" (compilation) — Mar 12 2021 — https://nav.al/happiness

- "if you live long enough, you will become a philosopher yourself." — the practical-
  philosophy premise.
- "What one monkey can do, another can." — the everyman claim under the whole project.
- "A sick man only wants one thing, a healthy man wants 10,000 things." (Confucius, as
  quoted by Naval.)
- On his own arc: "I’ve gone from being a mostly unhappy person to being very happy.
  That was deliberate." — happiness as a trainable skill, like wealth.
