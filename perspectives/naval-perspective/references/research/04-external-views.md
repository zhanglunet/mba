## A. Standing / Influence (framing — verify specifics before quoting)

- **Founder/investor standing.** Naval is co-founder of **AngelList** and, earlier,
  **Epinions** (his own account, stupid-games) and **Venture Hacks** (with Nivi).
  He is a widely followed angel investor. Specific portfolio names, check sizes,
  and returns are **not asserted here** — they require live verification and are
  out of scope for the judge.
- **The tweetstorm → podcast → book pipeline.** The 2018 "How to get rich (without
  getting lucky)" tweetstorm went viral, became the nav.al podcast (2019), and was
  compiled by **Eric Jorgenson** as *The Almanack of Naval Ravikant* (2020). The
  Almanack’s reach is the main vector of his popular influence. (See `02` §C — it
  is a **secondary compilation**, cited as an index, not a verbatim source.)
- **Reputation shape.** Naval is received publicly as a "philosopher-investor" /
  aphorist — the tech world’s Marcus-Aurelius-of-leverage. This framing is his
  brand’s outside face; the biographical specifics (birth in India, emigration to
  the US as a child) can be anchored to **https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naval_Ravikant**
  (secondary) if needed, and the "poor kid in India" self-description is first-party
  (money-luck, Mar 2019).

## B. Lines of Critique (attested themes — verify before quoting)

Recorded as *views that exist and are widely held*, not as sourced quotes.

- **Survivorship / hindsight bias.** The wealth advice is distilled by a man who
  already got rich; the same maxims in people who *stayed* poor don’t get a
  podcast. A standard critique of get-rich literature, Naval’s included. (Naval
  partly pre-empts it — "In 1,000 parallel universes, you want to be wealthy in 999
  of them" is an explicit attempt to factor luck out — but the critique that the
  claim is unfalsifiable stands.)
- **Aphorism vs. actionability.** Critics argue the maxims are memorable but
  under-operational ("specific knowledge" is defined partly by feel — "feels like
  play to me"), and that compression can flatten messy reality into poster-quotes.
  Naval himself concedes the risk: "If you don’t have the underlying experience,
  then it reads like a collection of quotes." (reject-advice) — use this
  first-party concession rather than inventing a hostile quote.
- **Politics / provocation.** Naval’s use of Trump/Kanye/socialists as examples,
  and his libertarian, techno-optimist, sometimes combative public persona, lead
  some readers to discount the ideas by association. For MBA purposes this is
  **out of scope** — the judge is built from the wealth/judgment/happiness corpus,
  not from the live X persona (parallels paulg’s X-persona carve-out).
- **Selection into leverage.** The advice assumes access (education, health,
  English, being "in the room") that Naval names as prerequisites ("There are some
  prerequisites you can’t get past") — critics note the ladder is easier to climb
  from some starting rungs. Again, first-party acknowledgment exists; no external
  quote is fabricated.

## C. How the outside view should shape the MBA judge

- Treat Naval’s frame as a **leverage / durability / judgment lens**, strongest on
  *why some businesses compound and some don’t* (network effects, zero marginal
  cost, long-term games) and weakest as a *deterministic personal-success formula*.
  The external critiques are correct that the personal maxims are hard to falsify;
  the SKILL encodes that as an explicit **limitation** per model.
- Keep the judge on the **published corpus**, off the live-X politics — more
  defensible and more useful.

## D. Anti-Fabrication Notes

- **No critic is quoted verbatim in this file.** Every critique is recorded as
  "this view exists / is widely held," with the specific wording marked *requires
  live verification*. Where a concession exists, it is quoted from **Naval’s own**
  first-party text (reject-advice), not manufactured for a critic.
- Biographical specifics (India origin, AngelList, Epinions dates) are Wikipedia-
  anchorable (secondary); dollar figures, valuations, and portfolio returns are
  **not asserted**.
- Do not manufacture a named detractor or a hostile quote. If balance requires
  one, fetch and verify it live.
