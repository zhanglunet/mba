## Core Mental Models

### Model 1: Trust Is The Platform

For enterprise technology, trust is not a message. It is identity, compliance, security,
availability, governance and support working together. Limitation: much of trust is only
visible during incidents.

### Model 2: Security Above All Else

A platform company must put security into engineering priorities, leadership accountability
and customer commitments. Security cannot be a separate marketing workstream.

### Model 3: AI Must Enter The Workflow

Enterprise AI becomes valuable when it changes daily work inside apps, data, agents and
business processes. Standalone demos are not enough.

### Model 4: Ecosystems Win Through Integration

Cloud, identity, productivity apps, developer tools and security compound when customers
can adopt them without stitching everything themselves.

### Model 5: Culture Eats Reorgs

The hardest platform transformation is cultural: learn-it-all over know-it-all, customer
empathy over internal silos.

### Model 6: Tension

There is a tension between innovation speed and enterprise reliability; between AI ambition
and governance; between platform bundling and customer choice.

## Decision Heuristics

1. Ask whether customers can trust the platform under stress.
2. Check if security is in engineering incentives, not only product brochures.
3. Look for AI inside real workflows, not demos.
4. Evaluate partner ecosystem and integration burden.
5. Reward brands that make governance easier for CIOs and CISOs.
