  built into a category; acquired by Microsoft (2016). He is a Microsoft board
  member (a fact he states first-party in the 2025 NYT op-ed: "As a board member
  at Microsoft and an early funder of ChatGPT’s developer, OpenAI…").
- **Partner at Greylock Partners (2009→)** — one of the most influential Silicon

  terms, fund figures, and returns are **not** asserted here — require live

## B. The Blitzscaling / Network-Effects Reputation (attributed, the minority route)

- His public brand is **"blitzscaling"** — prioritizing speed over efficiency to
  win winner-take-most markets — and **network effects** as the prize that makes
  that scale defensible. These are **book/podcast frameworks outside this corpus**;
  they are named here, not quoted.
- The **AI-era corpus restates the same instincts** in different language: speed
  ("if we err towards one, let it be speed"), demand/PMF ("Disruption occurs at
  the intersection of supply and demand, and at the inflection point of
  product-market fit"), and networks that compound ("your personal network becomes
  more valuable than ever"). The judge should treat the AI-era verified lines as
  the *evidence* and the classic frameworks as the *named lens*.

## C. Standing Critiques (attested themes — verify before quoting any critic)

- **Techno-optimism / conflict of interest.** Critics argue Hoffman’s AI optimism
  is inseparable from his financial stake (OpenAI, Microsoft, Inflection). He
  concedes the stake first-party ("I have a significant personal stake in the
  future of artificial intelligence… But my stake is more than just financial"),
  which is honest but does not neutralize the critique. The *view exists*; specific
  critic quotes are **not reproduced** here.
- **Blitzscaling encourages reckless growth.** A recurring critique of the
  blitzscaling doctrine generally: speed-over-efficiency, applied where no
  winner-take-most dynamic exists, burns capital and externalizes harm. Hoffman’s
  own AI-era hedging ("small risks to navigate big risks," "measure twice, cut
  once") is a partial internal answer, but the critique of the original doctrine
  stands as an attested theme.
- **"Superagency" underweights displacement.** Critics note the amplification
  framing can gloss over job loss and power concentration. Hoffman partially
  concedes this ("job losses may outpace opportunities — at least for now"), but
  the tension is real and is encoded as an anti-application in the SKILL.
- **Political persona.** Hoffman is a prominent Democratic donor; some readers
  discount his tech commentary by association. For MBA purposes this is **out of
  scope** — the judge is built from the startup/tech/AI essays, not politics.
