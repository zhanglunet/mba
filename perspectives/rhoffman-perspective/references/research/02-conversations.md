# Reid Hoffman — Conversations (Speeches, Podcasts, Interviews, AI Experiments)

**Status:** v1 — built 2026-07-07. The strongest first-party *spoken* record
readable in this pass is Hoffman’s **published speeches** (Bologna 2023, Perugia
2024, LSE 2024) — he delivered them and then posted the full text, so those are
quoted directly and verified against the corpus. His large podcast / interview
footprint (*Masters of Scale* especially) was **not transcript-readable** here
and is catalogued with dates + URLs and explicitly flagged; **no quotes are
reconstructed from it.**
**Scope:** talks, lectures, recorded interviews, podcasts, and his self-published
AI-avatar experiments. His written essays/op-eds are in `01-writings.md`.
**Cutoff:** 2026-05.

> **Honesty note.** Hoffman is one of the most prolific podcast voices in tech
> (he *hosts* two shows). But podcasts are audio/video-first; in this pass their
> transcripts were not text-readable, so per the SOP they are catalogued as
> "event confirmed, transcript not verified" — never quoted. The verbatim

## D. Conversational Register vs. Essay Register (for the SKILL author)

| Trait | In essays / op-eds | In speeches |
|---|---|---|
| Structure | Argument-first, sourced | Numbered maxims, rhetorical refrains ("Carthago…") |
| Register | Measured, systems-level | Warmer, more allusive (Hegel, cathedrals, griffins) |
| Signature move | The value-sculpting / GPS metaphor | The Latin maxim + historical parable |
| Optimism | Argued with trade-offs | Delivered as a charge to the audience |

The speech voice (Bologna, Perugia) is the same reasoning engine as the essays,
just more literary and more direct in address. For the MBA judge, the essay
register is the safer default; borrow the speech register only for the
"practical optimist" framing.

## E. Anti-Fabrication Notes

- The only verbatim conversational quotes used here come from speeches Hoffman
  published as text (A1–A3) and his own written notes on the avatar videos
  (B1–B2), all verified against the corpus.
- *Masters of Scale*, *Possible*, and interview appearances (C1–C3) are
  **confirmed events/channels** but their spoken lines were **not verified** —
  do not quote them. Reconstructing a plausible-sounding podcast quote is exactly
  the fabrication the SOP forbids.
- The classic blitzscaling / network-effects maxims live in books and podcasts
  outside this corpus; they are attributed by name, never quoted as verified.
