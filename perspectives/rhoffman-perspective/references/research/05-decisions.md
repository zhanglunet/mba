## A. The Founder Decision (attributed + one first-party line)

### A1. Co-found LinkedIn and create the professional-networking category — 2003

- What: Hoffman co-founded LinkedIn and bet on a then-unproven category:
  professional social networking, a two-sided network whose value compounds with
  each new member.
- Primary (thin, first-party): he confirms authorship of the bet — "I co-founded
  LinkedIn, a professional social network, more than two decades ago" (NYT, 2025)
  — and in his own avatar-video note frames the founding challenge: "The concept
  of professional networking was still untested, and our team faced the monumental
  challenge of creating a category." (2024)
- Result: LinkedIn became the dominant professional network and was acquired by
  Microsoft in 2016 (secondary — en.wikipedia.org; deal figure requires live
  verification).
- Lens it reveals: **network effects + category coinage** — build a two-sided
  network in a winner-take-most position. (The full blitzscaling reasoning is
  attributed, not quoted; see `04-external-views.md` §B.)

## B. The Investor / AI-Bet Decisions

### B1. Early-fund OpenAI and go all-in on the AI thesis — 2010s→2020s

- What: Hoffman became an early funder of OpenAI and a public champion of AI as
  the next general-purpose technology, while sitting on Microsoft’s board.
- Primary (first-party): "As a board member at Microsoft and an early funder of
  ChatGPT’s developer, OpenAI, I have a significant personal stake in the future
  of artificial intelligence." (NYT, 2025)
- Result: OpenAI/ChatGPT became the defining consumer-AI product — "It had 1
  million people using it in five days. And 100 million in 2 months." (Perugia,
  2024). Hoffman’s public brand pivoted to AI-optimism.
- Lens: **market timing** — "When new technology starts cresting, the best move is
  to surf that wave." (2025). He surfed the AI wave early and loudly.

### B2. Champion "iterative deployment" over regulatory pause as the governing doctrine — 2024

- What: Faced with calls to pause or heavily pre-regulate AI, Hoffman staked out a
  strategic position: release AI to the public at frequent cadence, learn in the
  open, and regulate by measuring.
- Primary: "the fastest and most effective way to develop safer, more equitable,
  and more useful AI tools is through iterative deployment." (LSE, 2024); "let’s
  regulate first by measuring." (LSE, 2024); "We must take small risks to navigate
  big risks." (LSE & Perugia, 2024).
- Result: a widely-cited framing of the deployment-vs-pause debate; also a
  self-interested position (he funds AI) — noted honestly in `04` §C.
- Lens: **ship-fast / small-risk iteration** — the AI-era restatement of his
  launch-and-learn instinct.

## C. The Practitioner / Builder Decisions (fully first-party, well-documented)

### C1. Clone his own voice to release the *Impromptu* audiobook — 2023

- What: Rather than spend days in a recording booth, Hoffman tested two voice-
  synthesis systems on his own voice — Vall-E (Microsoft research) and Respeecher
  (a Ukraine-based startup) — to narrate his book *Impromptu*.
- Primary: "These are both examples of how AI can help creators produce more,
  faster." (2023); paired with the caveat "opportunity also brings with it the
  need for responsibility and the potential for abuse." (2023).
- Result: he released Vall-E and Respeecher audio versions publicly as a
  demonstration; documented the trade-offs (intonation, deepfake risk) honestly.
- Lens: **hands-on experimentation** — he uses himself as the test case before
  advocating a technology.

### C2. Build "REID AI" and deliver a speech in 9 languages via his avatar — 2024

- What: Hoffman built an AI avatar (custom GPT-4 chatbot + Hour One / ElevenLabs)
  trained on his own corpus, then had it deliver the Perugia speech in nine
  languages he doesn’t speak, and translate the Bologna speech into 100+.
- Primary: the avatar’s persona "is generated from a custom chatbot built on GPT-4
  that was trained on my books, speeches, podcasts and other content that I’ve
  produced over the last few decades." (2024)
- Result: a proof-of-concept that AI can extend reach across language barriers —
  the thesis of the speeches, demonstrated by the medium.
- Lens: **the tool as demonstration** — "They are not our replacement, but a
  powerful extension of us." (Perugia, 2024).

## D. The Public-Intellectual Decision

### D1. Coin and campaign for "superagency" — 2024–2025

- What: Hoffman coined "superagency" and built a book (*Superagency*, 2025) and an
  essay/op-ed/speech campaign around reframing the AI debate from
  replacement-fear to agency-amplification.
- Primary: "AI offers heroic gains in human capability—a concept I’ve begun
  referring to as “superagency.”" (2024); "Superagency is what happens when a
  critical mass of individuals, personally empowered by AI, begin to operate at
  levels that compound throughout society." (2024).
- Result: "superagency" became his signature contribution to the AI discourse.
- Lens: **reframe the category** — like naming "blitzscaling," he coins the term
  that defines the debate on his terms.
