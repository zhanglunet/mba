## A. The Load-Bearing AI-Phase Texts

### A1. "AI, Society, and Our World Order" — 2024 — LSE lecture — https://www.reidhoffman.org/ai-society-and-our-world-order/

Delivered at the London School of Economics, December 2024. His most complete
statement of the AI worldview: value-sculpting, iterative deployment, small
risks, and geopolitics. The single richest source in the corpus (~32K chars).

- On what humans are: "We humans are Homo techne: humans as toolmakers and tool users."
- The tool-shaping loop: "We shape our tools. Then our tools shape us."
- On technology’s moral status: "So, is technology value-neutral or value laden? Neither. The truth is it’s value-sculpted."
- The sculpting metaphor (his signature move): "We shape it—not like clay, but like marble. It takes muscle, intention, and repetition."
- The risk doctrine (the AI-era restatement of ship-fast): "We must take small risks to navigate big risks."
- Why inaction is not safe: "stopping or pausing to avoid risk is a risk—and most often a more perilous one than embracing risk in the first place."
- The demand / PMF lens (his most VC-flavored line in the corpus): "Disruption occurs at the intersection of supply and demand, and at the inflection point of product-market fit."
- ...continued: "A technology disrupts when it resonates with people: when they adopt it, pay for it, and incorporate it into their lives. Without demand, even the most ambitious innovation falters."
- On regulation: "In short: let’s regulate first by measuring." and "Just that we find ways to measure twice, and cut once."
- On the West / Global South / China / Russia split: "In the West, the primary question is often, “Should we allow this?”"; "In the Global South, by contrast, the plea is more urgent: “Can you please include us?”"
- The speed verdict: "Striking the right balance between speed and deliberation is crucial, but if we err towards one, let it be speed."
- Minsky (stability→fragility): "stability creates instability".
- Inevitability with agency: "This transition to our AI future will be navigated regardless of our planning and coordination."

### A2. "Humanity’s Hegelian Golden Braid" — May 24, 2024 — Perugia honorary-doctorate speech — https://www.reidhoffman.org/perugia-speech/

Six Latin maxims, Hegel’s thesis→antithesis→synthesis, Hofstadter’s *Gödel,
Escher, Bach*, cathedrals, and the Perugian griffin. His most literary text.

- Maxim 1 (the founder’s creed): "Aut viam inveniam aut faciam. “I shall find a way or make one.”"
- On AI’s relation to us: "They are not our replacement, but a powerful extension of us."
- Symbols loop: "We shape our symbols and, in turn, they shape us."
- Maxim 4 (again the risk doctrine): "We must take small risks to navigate big risks."
- The build-by-building thesis: "To figure out what to build, they simply started building."
- On synthesis: "The synthesis is not a cyborg, but a better human."
- The refrain that closes the speech: "It’s not human vs. AI. It’s human with AI." / "It’s not thesis vs. antithesis. It’s synthesis." / "We must shape AI. And, in turn, let AI help shape us."
- On fear vs. hope: "we don’t need to forget fear, but we do need to start navigating with hope."
- The racetrack metaphor for speed (Dallara): "To maintain stability in a turn, you’ve got to accelerate through it."
- Quoting Patrick Collison on the internet-as-city: "Several billion people recently immigrated to earth’s most vibrant city."
- ChatGPT adoption speed: "It had 1 million people using it in five days. And 100 million in 2 months."
- Maxim 6 (the obligation): "we have an obligation to make the future better than the now."

### A3. "Superagency" — 2024 — LinkedIn essay — https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/superagency-reid-hoffman-d3ojf/

The concept essay (also the title of his 2025 book *Superagency*, which is
attributed but not quoted here). Frames AI as a "super-tool" that compounds
human agency across society.

- Coinage: "AI offers heroic gains in human capability—a concept I’ve begun referring to as “superagency.”"
- Definition: "Superagency is what happens when a critical mass of individuals, personally empowered by AI, begin to operate at levels that compound throughout society."
- On intelligence as leverage: "Intelligence itself is now a tool—a scalable, highly configurable, self-compounding engine for progress."
- The tool-maker throughline: "And now we have a new super-tool: AI."
- The leap framing: "AI will enable our next great leap forward."

### A4. "Don’t fear AI: used well, it can empower us all" — 2024 — The Times (UK) — https://www.thetimes.com/comment/columnists/article/dont-fear-ai-used-well-it-can-empower-us-all-hpzrg9xsd

Built on Isaiah Berlin’s *Two Concepts of Liberty* (negative vs. positive
liberty). The most rigorous articulation of "superagency" as a political-theory
idea.

- Definition (public-good framing): "Superagency, as I define it, is what happens when large numbers of people get access to a transformative, general-purpose technology that they’re free to use as they wish."
- The steam-power analogy: "we’re in the midst of a new steam power moment."
- Value-pluralism honesty: "the work of civilisation involves trade-offs."
- The hands-on-trust argument (his adoption thesis): "“What’s in it for me?” leads naturally to “What’s in it for us?”"
- ChatGPT as GPS: "I think of tools like ChatGPT as a new form of informational GPS."
- Agency as the root concern: "Questions about individual agency lie at the heart of most of the major concerns about AI."

### A5. "A.I. Will Empower Humanity" — Jan 25, 2025 — NYT op-ed — https://www.nytimes.com/2025/01/25/opinion/ai-chatgpt-empower-bot.html

The individual-empowerment case, anchored in his LinkedIn experience and the
idea of a data-driven "second self."

- Thesis: "I truly believe that by giving billions of people access to A.I. tools they can use in whatever ways they choose, we can create a world where A.I. augments and amplifies human creativity and labor instead of simply replacing it."
- The share-more argument: "sharing more information in more contexts can also improve people’s lives."
- The one first-party LinkedIn identity line in the corpus: "I co-founded LinkedIn, a professional social network, more than two decades ago".
- On recall as direction: "perfect recall isn’t just a tool for remembering the past. It’s also a compass".

### A6. "No, AI isn’t coming to destroy us. But it will transform the world" — Apr 13, 2025 — The San Francisco Standard — https://sfstandard.com/opinion/2025/04/13/ai-isnt-coming-to-destroy-us-but-it-will-transform-the-world/

Reframes the AGI debate into a usefulness question, then forecasts memory +
multimodality.

- The reframe: "Will AI be significantly more useful, more powerful, and more integrated into every aspect of life three years from now?"
- The trajectory: "interacting with AI in 2028 will feel as qualitatively different from today as today does from 2022".
- The role shift: "the shift is underway from autocomplete to coauthor, from database to chatbot to trusted confidante and creative partner".

### A7. "Move fast and make things: the new career mantra" — Jun 15, 2025 — The San Francisco Standard — https://sfstandard.com/opinion/2025/06/15/move-fast-and-make-things/

His clearest *Start-up-of-You*-lineage text in the corpus: career advice for the
AI age. (The book itself is attributed, not quoted.)

- The optimism anchor: "I’m more bullish than most on the future of human labor."
- Dynamic over static (the core Start-up-of-You move): "What you really want is a dynamic career path, not a static one."
- Market timing, personal edition: "When new technology starts cresting, the best move is to surf that wave."
- The reframe: "So if you’re a recent graduate, I urge you not to think in terms of AI-proofing your career. Instead, AI-optimize it." / "AI is a tool you can master."
- Experiments over plans: "Instead of making five-year plans, consider six-month experiments."
- Networks compound (the network-effects lens, career edition): "Human referrals and trust can’t scale like AI, so your personal network becomes more valuable than ever." / "personal relationships have never been more valuable".
- Agency filter: "The ability to self-start, to build and act without credentials, is ultimately what will get you hired."
- The closing charge: "Be the person who moves early, learns fast, and brings others with you."
- "So become AI fluent but focus on people. Foster even more relationships."
- "Learn in public: Let people see the journey you’re on."

### A8. Bologna Business School Commencement Speech — Sep 8, 2023 — https://www.reidhoffman.org/bologna-business-school-commencement-speech/

His clearest "theory of human nature underlying a venture" text — the closest
the corpus comes to his founder-and-investor operating philosophy.

- On what a venture needs: "any new venture must have a strong theory of human nature underlying it."
- ...and why: "you need to know about humanity: the everyday person’s wants and needs."
- The mission framing: "This is the highest calling of business."
- The accelerationist line: "That’s why the answer is not to slow down technology, but to accelerate it."
- ...continued: "Technology is a tool. And the faster we have it in our hands, the better we can solve the problems we have—and the problems it might create."
- The McLuhan/Culkin quote he leans on: "“We become what we behold. We shape our tools and then our tools shape us.”"
- The generation naming: "You are the AI generation. You are Generation AI."
- His preferred expansion of the acronym: "AI should not be an abbreviation for artificial intelligence, but for amplification intelligence".
- Homo techne (recurs across texts): "A more accurate name for us is Homo techne" / "The story of humanity is the story of technology."
- On timing: "the future is always sooner and stranger than you might think."
- On AI as orientation tool: "A steam engine of the mind. A cognitive GPS."

### A9. "AI voice synthesis tech 🤝 Impromptu" — 2023 — https://www.reidhoffman.org/ai-voice-synthesis-tech-impromptu/

A first-person builder log: testing Vall-E (Microsoft) and Respeecher on his own
voice for the *Impromptu* audiobook. Useful as evidence of hands-on experimentation.

- Creator-leverage takeaway: "These are both examples of how AI can help creators produce more, faster."
- Responsibility caveat: "opportunity also brings with it the need for responsibility and the potential for abuse."
