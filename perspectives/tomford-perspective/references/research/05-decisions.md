## Decision heuristics (≥5, each traceable to a verified line)

### H1 — Be obsessive; control the details, shape, cut, color `[GQ]`

> Yes, I think I am obsessive when I design. I'm very meticulous `[GQ]`
> I'm obsessive in details and shape and cut and color `[GQ]`

**anti-application (limitation):** obsessive single-author control is hard to scale or hand off (Tension A); perfection can stall.

### H2 — Endow the product with excitement; it must make you stop `[GQ]`

> when you're excited when you're designing something, I think that you can endow the product with that same excitement `[GQ]`
> I think it has to make you stop `[GQ]`

**anti-application (blind spot):** "makes you stop" measures attention, not durability or use-value.

### H3 — Looking your best is a show of respect; be part of the visual landscape `[GQ]`

> it isn't glamour, it's just the way I like things to look `[GQ]`
> It's a show of respect to other people to look your best `[GQ]`

**anti-application (limitation):** a taste-and-respect frame is a point of view, not a business case.

### H4 — Aim to be part of your time; capture the moment `[GQ]`

> which I think is the goal, is to be part of your time `[GQ]`
> I think it hopefully means that I captured that moment in time `[GQ]`

**anti-application (blind spot):** being "of your time" risks dating; iconic-in-hindsight is not guaranteed at the moment of making.

### H5 — Put the clothes over the spectacle; keep a classic core `[GQ]`

> I want people to be close to them and not be distracted by anything but the man or the woman and the clothes `[GQ]`
> my core is quite classic `[GQ]`

**anti-application (limitation):** the anti-spectacle stance is a personal preference; spectacle can also build a brand.

### H6 — Make people beautiful (not "sexy"); think global, know yourself `[IV][GQ]`

> It's my business to make a woman or a man beautiful `[IV]`
> I think today you have to be international and global. It's very narrow to think in a nationalistic way `[IV]`
> it's the ones who really know themselves `[GQ]`（最会穿的人)

**anti-application (blind spot):** "beauty not sex" is his stated intent; the market often reads (and buys) the sex — intent ≠ reception.

## Cases / context (era-bound, see `04`)

- **Gucci/YSL revival (1990s–2004):** the perfectionist total-control image-maker; "I captured that moment in time."
- **A Single Man / 2010 womenswear return:** protested fashion-as-impersonal, showed to ~100 guests, "see the clothes."
- **CFDA chairman (2019–):** "help American fashion become more internationally known"; global > nationalistic.
- **Sold Tom Ford to Estée Lauder (2022):** the brand is now group-owned; post-2022 direction is not his (cutoff note).

## Anti-fabrication notes

- Heuristics quote only verified first-party lines; anti-application + tensions given for each.
- Provocative sexual-politics lines are **not** used as heuristics (see `04`).
- Self-conflict (his own houses / Estée Lauder) carried into SKILL.
