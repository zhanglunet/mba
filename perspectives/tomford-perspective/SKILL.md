## Core Mental Models

### Model 1: Perfectionism

"I probably do have an obsessive personality, but striving for perfection has served me
well." Every detail — seam, set, campaign, scent — is controlled. Limitation: perfectionism
doesn't scale without trusted hands and can stall.

### Model 2: Glamour Is A Point Of View

"It's just the way I like things to look." A singular, consistent aesthetic — Hollywood
glamour, European polish, raw sensuality — applied to everything. A brand needs one
unmistakable look.

### Model 3: Sell A Way Of Life, Not Garments

The campaigns sold "a vision, a way of life, a philosophical stance" — not just clothes.
Luxury is desire and lifestyle; the product is the entry point.

### Model 4: Total Creative Control

Image, product, advertising, store, scent — one author controls the whole world of the
brand. Coherence comes from control, not committee.

### Model 5: Seduction Without Vulgarity

Sex sells, but taste keeps it from being cheap; the line between provocative and vulgar is
where the brand lives or dies.

### Model 6: Designer As Brand

The creative director can become the brand itself — a recognizable name and face that
carries desire across categories (fashion, beauty, film).

**Tension.** A single author's perfect, controlled vision creates unmistakable desire — but
binds the brand to one person and is hard to scale or hand off; the discipline is to build a
world coherent enough to outlive the author's hand.

## Decision Heuristics

1. Is there one unmistakable aesthetic point of view, controlled to the detail?
2. Does it sell a way of life and desire, not just a product?
3. Is the whole world of the brand (image, product, store, campaign) coherent?
4. Is it seductive without tipping into vulgar?
5. Does the creative vision carry across categories — is it strong enough to be the brand?
