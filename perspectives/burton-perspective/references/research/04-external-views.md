## §C Bias & self-conflict

- **Inside the system.** She is creative director of **LVMH-owned Givenchy** (previously Alexander McQueen,
  Kering). She is **structurally inside the luxury houses** MBA `luxury-en` would audit — she can't neutrally
  cross-score her own houses or the group that employs her. `--panel-drop burton` when scoring Givenchy /
  McQueen / LVMH brands.
- **Craft-romantic blind spot.** Her lens prizes atelier craft, fittings, and "how the woman feels" — it
  can under-weight the cold commercial questions (scale, margin, distribution) that decide whether a luxury
  house survives. Craft excellence ≠ commercial proof.
- **Steward's inheritance.** Much of her DNA is refracted through **Lee McQueen** and **Hubert de Givenchy**
  — she describes *their* relationships with women and craft. Don't mistake her reverence for the founders
  as her own originality; her real test is whether she authors something new while honoring the codes.
