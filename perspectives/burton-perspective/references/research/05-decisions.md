## Decision heuristics (≥5, each traceable to a verified line)

### H1 — Go to the atelier first; the hands that make the clothes are the soul `[ANO][WWD2]`

> To go forward, you have to go back to the beginning `[ANO]`
> You had a constant conversation with the ateliers and you realized how important are the hands that make the clothes `[WWD2]`

**anti-application (limitation):** atelier craft is slow, costly, hard to scale; the soul of a house still has to survive commercially (Tension A).

### H2 — Judge it on the body, in the fitting — not on the sketch `[WWD]`

> But really it comes alive in the fitting `[WWD]`
> I ask the model to walk. I cut the fabric, I drape, and only then can you experiment in proportion and shape `[WWD]`

**anti-application (limitation):** a fitting-first test rewards construction, not necessarily desirability or price-value.

### H3 — Ask how the woman *feels* — powerful, but still herself `[WWD]`

> It's a form of armor that the women are putting on `[WWD]`
> I want them to feel very Givenchy, but I also want them to feel like themselves so they're not overpowered by the clothes `[WWD]`

**anti-application (blind spot):** an emotional-experience test is hard to measure and can rationalize away weak commercials.

### H4 — Design look-by-look, with a couture mindset and a real woman in mind `[WWD]`

> When I design a collection, I design it very much look by look `[WWD]`
> it's almost like I am doing a couture collection `[WWD]`

**anti-application (limitation):** couture-level care per look is the opposite of scalable ready-to-wear economics.

### H5 — Create out of constraint; it was never 'no'; don't be too precious `[WWD][WWD2]`

> There was this energy of creating often out of nothing `[WWD]`
> It also taught me it was never 'no. `[WWD]`
> if you're too precious about things, you don't experiment `[WWD2]`

**anti-application (blind spot):** "out of nothing" is a founder-scrappiness memory; a mature house has different constraints.

### H6 — Steward the DNA, but speak to the woman of today `[WWD]`

> you can't pretend to be somebody you're not `[WWD]`
> you have to adapt to what you want to say to women today `[WWD]`
> it's important to go back to the DNA of the house `[WWD]`

**anti-application (blind spot):** the balance is delicate — over-honor the founder and you imitate; under-honor and you erase (Tension B).

## Cases / context (register-bound, see `04`)

- **McQueen (26 years, 13 as CD):** hand-stitching in the Hoxton Square basement; tea-dyeing £2/meter fabric;
  "beautiful when there was no money as when there was money" — her formative school of resourceful craft.
- **Givenchy (from Sept 2024):** in-house ateliers ("see the clothes growing"); honoring Hubert's relationships
  with women (Audrey Hepburn, Bunny Mellon) while designing for women today.
