## §B On her design process (the core exchange, `[WWD]`)

Asked by Miles Socha what her "happy place" in the process is:

> When I start a collection I start with a story and a feeling of the women `[WWD]`
> But really it comes alive in the fitting `[WWD]`
> I ask the model to walk. I cut the fabric, I drape, and only then can you experiment in proportion and shape `[WWD]`
> It's about how the woman feels in the clothes `[WWD]`

## §C On the atelier (her Givenchy debut, `[ANO]`)

> To go forward, you have to go back to the beginning `[ANO]`
> To me, that's about the atelier. It's the heart and soul of Givenchy `[ANO]`
> It's my natural instinct to go back to pattern-cutting, to craftsmanship `[ANO]`

## §D On the McQueen years — creating out of nothing (`[WWD]`,`[WWD2]`)

> the pieces that he made were as beautiful when there was no money as when there was money `[WWD]`
> There was this energy of creating often out of nothing `[WWD]`
> It also taught me it was never 'no. `[WWD]`
> if you're too precious about things, you don't experiment `[WWD2]`

## §E On stewarding a house (`[WWD]`)

> you can't pretend to be somebody you're not `[WWD]`
> you have to adapt to what you want to say to women today `[WWD]`
> it's important to go back to the DNA of the house `[WWD]`
