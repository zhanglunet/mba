## §A The atelier is the heart and soul — go back to the beginning `[ANO][WWD]`

Her deepest principle: craft and the hands that make the clothes are where a house actually lives.

> To go forward, you have to go back to the beginning `[ANO]`
> To me, that's about the atelier. It's the heart and soul of Givenchy `[ANO]`
> It's my natural instinct to go back to pattern-cutting, to craftsmanship `[ANO]`
> It's what I feel, how I work, and want to do `[ANO]`
> You had a constant conversation with the ateliers and you realized how important are the hands that make the clothes `[WWD2]`
> you can see the clothes growing as you're creating them `[WWD]`（论 Givenchy in-house ateliers)

## §B It comes alive in the fitting — on the body, not the sketch `[WWD]`

Design is three-dimensional and emotional; it is not a flat sketch or a render.

> When I start a collection I start with a story and a feeling of the women `[WWD]`
> But really it comes alive in the fitting `[WWD]`
> I love to drape, I love to make clothes for women that are not too two-dimensional `[WWD]`
> I ask the model to walk. I cut the fabric, I drape, and only then can you experiment in proportion and shape `[WWD]`

## §C How the woman feels — armor + feel like herself `[WWD]`

The test is not spectacle but the wearer's experience: powerful, but still herself.

> It's about how the woman feels in the clothes `[WWD]`
> when you put something on, it's about how you feel as a woman `[WWD]`
> Fashion is the biggest form of self-expression `[WWD]`
> It's a form of armor that the women are putting on `[WWD]`
> I want them to feel very Givenchy, but I also want them to feel like themselves so they're not overpowered by the clothes `[WWD]`
> It's almost like a conversation between the two `[WWD]`

## §D Design look-by-look — a couture mindset, the woman in mind `[WWD][WWD2]`

> When I design a collection, I design it very much look by look `[WWD]`
> it's almost like I am doing a couture collection `[WWD]`
> It taught Lee, and then me, how to do a collection almost like a couture collection `[WWD2]`
> It starts with the silhouette `[WWD2]`
> I love being a dressmaker `[WWD]`

## §E Creating out of nothing — it was never 'no'; don't be too precious `[WWD][WWD2]`

Resourceful craft: constraint fuels invention; quality is independent of budget.

> the pieces that he made were as beautiful when there was no money as when there was money `[WWD]`
> We bought fabric when it was two pounds a meter. We tea dyed it, we cut it up `[WWD]`
> There was this energy of creating often out of nothing `[WWD]`
> It also taught me it was never 'no. `[WWD]`
> if you're too precious about things, you don't experiment `[WWD2]`

## §F Learned everything hands-on from Lee — make the job your own `[WWD][WWD2]`

> I learned how to pattern cut and learned how to sew. I learned how to think out of the box, be creative `[WWD]`
> you make the job your own `[WWD]`
> it was always this self-training to bring the dream alive `[WWD]`
> I started in the basement in Hoxton Square `[WWD]`
> I remember how much Lee appreciated the ateliers, how much he learned from them `[WWD2]`

## §G Authenticity + steward the DNA, evolve for today `[WWD][WWD2]`

Inheriting a founder's house: honor its DNA, but don't imitate — speak to the woman of today.

> you can't pretend to be somebody you're not `[WWD]`
> you have to adapt to what you want to say to women today `[WWD]`
> it's important to go back to the DNA of the house `[WWD]`
> It should have an identity to it, that when it's on a hanger you know it's a Givenchy jacket `[WWD]`
> I think it's important to establish the sort of clothes and things that are authentic to who you are `[WWD]`
> it's silhouette, it's feminine, it has to have a beauty to it `[WWD]`
> to me, the cut is the most important thing `[WWD]`
