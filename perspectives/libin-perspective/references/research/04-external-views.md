## View 1 — NIO's User Enterprise Is Loved And Costly

External observers often admire NIO's user community and service system while
questioning whether the heavy service / swap / NIO House model can produce
durable profitability.

Source anchors:

- NIO 2019 20-F / SEC filing on NIO Power and swap network:
  https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1736541/000110465920061585/nio-20191231x20f.htm
- NIO annual reports / user enterprise language:
  https://nio.gcs-web.com/static-files/6b22c48b-d08c-44d4-b592-bbc69b390293
- CnEVPost battery swap interview:
  https://cnevpost.com/2020/11/02/nio-founder-addresses-misconceptions-about-battery-swap-mode/

MBA implication:

- **Leverage quality:** service infrastructure is leverage only if usage density
  and economics improve.
- **Identity coherence:** NIO's service promise is core identity, not a perk.

## View 2 — 2019 Crisis Is The Reality Check

The "2019 most difficult moment" prevents the Li Bin judge from romanticizing
community. NIO had layoffs, recall pressure and cash strain; Hefei support was
a survival node, not decorative partnership.

Source anchors:

- People's Daily Auto internal-letter report:
  https://auto.people.com.cn/n1/2019/0823/c1005-31312330.html
- Autohome post-crisis dialogue:
  https://www.autohome.com.cn/news/202009/1043305.html

MBA implication:

- Brands that claim long-term user commitment must show what they protect when
  capital is tight.

## View 3 — Battery Swap Is Strategic But Contested

Battery swap creates strong user experience and differentiation, but external
questions remain around capex, standardization, alliance adoption and overseas
fit.

MBA implication:

- Reward infrastructure only when it has real usage, partner adoption or
  service economics.
- Do not assume every market accepts the same energy-service model.

## External Guardrails

1. Do not treat user community as automatically profitable.
2. Do not treat service cost as waste without examining retention / trust.
3. Always ask what the company refuses to cut during crisis.
