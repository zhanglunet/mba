# Sal Khan — Key decisions (what he did + why)

**Status:** v1 — built 2026-07-07. Each decision carries the move and source. Self-narrated decisions cite
the TED talks (primary); numbers need live verification. **cutoff:** 2026-05. Sources: 2011 / 2023 TED.

---

## A. Publish the tutoring videos publicly and for free (2011, self-narrated)

- Move: what began as private videos for his cousins he made public and free; kept going after letters
  (e.g., a child with autism who "got through") showed it was "more than just a nice-to-have."
  > it's all free, not trying to sell anything.（2011)
- Result: a permanent, scalable library.
  > this content will never grow old（2011)
- Lens: **access first** — free, world-class content that compounds over time (nonprofit, not a sell).

## B. Quit the hedge fund; build a nonprofit (2011, self-narrated)

- Move: left finance to turn Khan Academy into "a real organization -- we're a not-for-profit."
- Lens: **mission over monetization** — chose a nonprofit structure for a public good.
- Limit (honest): the talks don't address how a free nonprofit funds frontier-model AI (see 04 B).

## C. Design for mastery, not coverage (2011)

- Move: exercises that require "10 in a row" before moving on; a knowledge map that gates advanced topics
  on mastered foundations — the opposite of "70/80/90% then move on."
  > We encourage you to experiment. We encourage you to fail. But we do expect mastery.（2011)
- Lens: **fill the Swiss-cheese gaps** — mastery-based progression over snapshot exams.

## D. Flip the classroom to humanize it (2011)

- Move: promote teachers' practice of assigning lectures as homework and doing "homework" in class,
  freeing class time for human interaction.
  > these teachers have used technology to humanize the classroom.（2011)
- Lens: **tech removes the one-size-fits-all lecture so humans can do the human part.**

## E. Build Khanmigo with guardrails, not a cheating tool (2023, self-narrated — his product)

- Move: rather than ban ChatGPT, build an AI tutor that is Socratic, recorded/moderated, and refuses to
  give answers; and make it "think before it speaks" to raise math/tutoring accuracy.
  > It is not a cheating tool.（2023)
  > we could dramatically improve its ability in math and its ability in tutoring if we allow the AI to think before it speaks.（2023)
  > the AI doesn't write for you, it writes with you.（2023)
- Lens: **augment, don't replace**; give teacher time back to students.
- Limit (self-conflict): this is **his own product**; capability claims are marketing, demos curated (see 04/Self-Conflict).

## F. Take an optimistic-but-vigilant public stance on AI (2023)

- Move: argue against a fear-driven pause (which he says only helps "rule breakers"), while insisting on
  guardrails and reasonable regulation, and framing AI as a way to enhance human intelligence.
  > we fight like hell for the positive use cases.（2023)
- Lens: **active participants, not spectators** — shape AI toward the positive.

## G. Decision-rationale patterns (= the operating system)

| Pattern | Decisions |
|---|---|
| **Access first, free & compounding** | Public free videos (A) |
| **Mission over monetization (nonprofit)** | Quit finance, nonprofit (B) |
| **Mastery over coverage** | 10-in-a-row, knowledge map (C) |
| **Tech to humanize, not automate away** | Flip the classroom (D); teacher time back (E) |
| **Augment with guardrails, not replace** | Khanmigo Socratic/non-cheating (E) |
| **Optimistic but vigilant on AI** | Fight for positive use cases (F) |
