# Sal Khan — Expression DNA (language style, anchored to verified quotes)

**Status:** v1 — built 2026-07-07. Each trait anchored to a verified quote from `01`/`02` (2011 / 2023 TED).
⚠️ Sample is two prepared TED talks — an optimistic, demo-driven register; not the full range.
**cutoff:** 2026-05.

---

## Sentence patterns

- **Concrete analogy to make an abstraction stick** — the bicycle for mastery, "Swiss cheese gaps" for
  incomplete foundations, the unicycle joke for moving students on before mastery.
  > learn math the way you'd learn anything, like riding a bicycle.（2011)
- **Triad of imperatives** — parallel short clauses.
  > We encourage you to experiment. We encourage you to fail. But we do expect mastery.（2011)

## Vocabulary

- Signature terms: **mastery, self-paced, flip the classroom, 2 sigma, guardrails, Socratic, personal
  tutor, teaching assistant, humanize**.
  > these teachers have used technology to humanize the classroom.（2011)

## Tone

- **Earnest, mission-first, optimistic** — frames technology as service, not product; downplays the sell.
  > it's all free, not trying to sell anything.（2011)
- **Optimistic-but-vigilant on AI** — pairs big upside with "guardrails" and a call to act.
  > we fight like hell for the positive use cases.（2023)

## Rhythm

- Fixed arc: **anecdote / letter → the counterintuitive insight → scale it to everyone → a moral close.**
  Typical: cousins prefer YouTube → self-paced removes embarrassment → flip the classroom for everyone.

## Rhetorical habit

- **Demo-driven proof** — shows a live example, then generalizes ("This isn't a fake demo").
- **Coinage / reframing** — turns a "problem" into an "opportunity"; AI to enhance HI.
  > addressing the 2 sigma problem and turning it into a 2 sigma opportunity, dramatically accelerating education as we know it.（2023)
  > if AI, artificial intelligence, can be used to enhance HI, human intelligence, human potential and human purpose.（2023)

## Citation habit

- Anchors big claims to a credible source — Benjamin Bloom's 1984 "2 sigma" study. **This concept is
  Bloom's, credited, not his own** (see 01 §E).
